# 2026-04-05/06: v5 Sweep (16 configs, PSF-consistent)

## Key Finding: Oracle Data Labelled Differently

Discovered that the golden/oracle trajectory data was labelled with a **different pipeline** than the training data:

| Aspect | Training Data | Oracle/Golden Data |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|
| Gemini model | gemini-2.5-flash | gemini-3.1-flash-lite-preview |
| Prompt mode | Oracle (sees future 15 steps) | Predict-only (no future evidence) |
| Qwen embedding | Qwen3-8B, layer 30 | Qwen3-8B, layer 30 (same) |

Cross-dataset cosine similarity is 0.98 so embeddings land in similar space, but semantic content differs (factual summary vs forecast). This OOD mismatch likely explains:
- Why higher oracle_loss_weight hurt gameplay (more OOD oracle data → more confusion)
- Why v4_bw01_drop03 (lowest oracle weight) was best for gameplay

**Key observations from tasks 0-5:**

- Lower LR (1e-4) gives best discrimination (Δ=+0.47 vs +0.41 at 3e-4 vs +0.24 at 1e-3)
- acc_real ~0.97 is saturated — headroom is in pushing acc_zero lower
- acc_shuf ~0.87 appropriately between real and zero — PSF-consistent data fixes the v4 problem
