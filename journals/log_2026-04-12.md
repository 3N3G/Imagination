### Hypothesis verdict

- **freeze_obs_bcawr (16.80) matches/beats AWR baseline (16.30)** — freezing the obs pathway while running BC+AWR successfully protects against BC's obs-pathway corruption. This is the first BC+AWR configuration that does not collapse return — previously BC+AWR unfrozen collapsed from 16→7.46 (-8.84).
- **freeze_all_bcawr (14.68) slightly worse than AWR** — restricting BC to only `hidden_fc1 + ln2` still avoids collapse. Golden acc 64.95% is the lowest of the 4 configs, consistent with the smaller channel limiting what BC can over-memorize.
- **Both BC-only configs (freeze_obs_bc=5.54, freeze_all_bc=8.56) remain broken** — BC without AWR continues to be toxic even when restricted. freeze_obs_bc golden acc = **97.90%** (vs 30.71% on held-out training): BC fully memorizes oracle embeddings but fails to transfer to online.
- **Memorization signal is clear on golden (BC training set) but not on held-out**: all 4 freeze configs show massive real-vs-zero gaps on golden (43-60% acc) because golden is BC's training distribution. On held-out (offset=126, PPO trajectory distribution), gaps are much smaller (1.5-9%), confirming BC has not generalized its oracle→action rule to the broader distribution.
- Held-out NLL is best for freeze_obs_bcawr (1.90) and freeze_all_bcawr (1.91), both with AWR; worst for freeze_obs_bc (7.64, signaling training data unfamiliar to a BC-only pretrained policy). Return ordering matches NLL ordering.

## Experiment 3: Embedding-Source Comparison (pure AWR, PSF)

**Question.** Our original pipeline embeds Gemini-generated narrative text by taking Qwen3-8B's layer-30 hidden state (a generative model repurposed as an encoder). Do dedicated embedding models produce cleaner, more semantic representations of the same narratives than this layer-30 readout? Three encoders compared, same PSF labels, pure AWR (no BC):

1. **qwen3gen** — Qwen3-8B layer-30 hidden (original, 4096-dim)
2. **qwen3emb** — Qwen3-Embedding-8B last-token pool (dedicated embedding model, 4096-dim)
3. **gemini_emb** — Gemini embedding-001 (dedicated embedding model, 3072-dim) *[pending]*

### Online returns (50 ep)

| Encoder | Return | vs unaug (18.38 ± 2.69) |
|---------|--------|------------------------|
| unaug (no embedding) | 18.38 ± 2.69 | — |
| qwen3gen (original)  | 18.62 ± 3.20 | +0.24 |
| qwen3emb             | 17.86 ± 3.84 | -0.52 |
| gemini_emb (3072-d)  | 18.80 ± 2.69 | +0.42 |

All three encoders land within one noise band of the no-embedding baseline. No encoder distinguishes itself.

### Held-out validation (real / zero / shuffled)

| Encoder | real NLL | zero NLL | shuf NLL | real acc | zero acc | shuf acc |
|---------|----------|----------|----------|----------|----------|----------|
| qwen3gen   | 2.2384 | 2.2640 | 2.2524 | 42.98% | 42.39% | 42.63% |
| qwen3emb   | 2.1893 | 2.2156 | 2.2223 | 44.18% | 43.56% | 43.35% |
| gemini_emb | 2.1770 | 2.2019 | 2.1970 | 44.26% | 43.73% | 43.77% |

Real-vs-zero gap is <1% accuracy / ≤+0.03 NLL across all three encoders — the policy barely uses the embedding regardless of source. Golden validation was skipped for gemini_emb (oracle = 4096-dim Qwen3-8B, incompatible with 3072-dim Gemini embeddings).

### Golden validation

| Encoder | real NLL | zero NLL | shuf NLL | real acc | zero acc | shuf acc |
|---------|----------|----------|----------|----------|----------|----------|
| qwen3gen | 5.6533 | 5.6875 | 5.6443 | 8.70% | 9.55% | 8.93% |
| qwen3emb | 5.7242 | 5.7766 | 5.7243 | 9.42% | 9.44% | 9.42% |

All accuracies 8-10%; real/zero/shuffled indistinguishable. Human trajectories are OOD for both embedding distributions.

### Verdict

- **No embedding source beats the no-embedding baseline under pure AWR.** The "dedicated embedding models capture semantics better than generative-model hidden states" hypothesis is not supported.
- **The policy essentially ignores the embedding.** Real-vs-zero validation deltas are <1% accuracy on held-out and ~0% on golden, regardless of encoder. Whatever semantic content each encoder captures, the AWR policy routes around it.
- Under pure AWR, obs alone carries the signal. The embedding pathway is quiescent at validation — its influence on online return is also within noise (±0.5 vs baseline). This matches Apr 8: the imagination pathway amplifies BC toxicity but doesn't drive AWR behavior either way.
- **What this leaves open:** encoder quality may matter more under BC+AWR (where the imagination pathway actively shapes actions), or under prompt variants that produce more policy-relevant narratives. This experiment only closes the question for pure AWR + PSF.

## Experiment 4: Adversarial-Prompt Content Test on freeze_obs_bcawr

Causal test — does the `freeze_obs_bcawr` policy's 16.80-return depend on the *content* of Gemini's narrative, or only on the *presence* of a non-zero hidden-state embedding? Re-ran 50-ep online eval with `--embedding-mode {die, adversarial}` suffixes that instruct Gemini to predict death-seeking / worst-play futures. Same checkpoint, same Qwen embedder, only the Gemini prompt changed.

| Mode | Gemini prompt | Return | vs baseline (gemini mode) |
|------|---------------|--------|----------------------------|
| gemini (baseline)  | predict plausible next 15 steps | 16.80 ± 3.49 | — |
| die                | predict the player dying in next 15 turns | 16.00 ± 3.27 | **-0.80** |
| adversarial        | predict worst-play self-sabotaging behavior | 16.00 ± 3.30 | **-0.80** |

### Verdict

- **Adversarial and die prompts do not meaningfully change return.** Both drop by exactly 0.80 (well within the 3.3 std).
- Combined with the small held-out real-vs-zero gap (-6.62% acc, +0.61 NLL), **this confirms the policy is NOT listening to narrative content**. It uses the presence of some non-zero embedding as a mild conditioning signal, but the specific semantic message doesn't steer behavior.
- This is consistent with the embedding-source null result above: if the policy ignores *what* each encoder captures, asking Gemini for malicious futures also leaves behavior intact. The imagination pathway in `freeze_obs_bcawr` appears to provide a content-agnostic regularization / warm-up signal, not a planning input.
- **Remaining open question**: does *any* policy architecture genuinely use the narrative? The freeze_obs_bcawr real-vs-zero gap (6.62%) is the largest we've seen for a non-toxic policy, so this result is a relative lower bound on narrative use — weaker pathways would use it even less.

## Experiment 5: freeze_obs_bcawr 200K-Step Extended Training

Same config as `freeze_obs_bcawr` (pretrained AWR + freeze obs_branch + BC+AWR fine-tune, OF=0.05, OW=0.5, LR=1e-4), but 200K steps instead of 50K. Tests whether extended BC-in-a-contained-pathway can push past unaug (18.38).

| Metric | freeze_obs_bcawr (50K) | freeze_obs_bcawr_long (200K) | Δ |
|--------|------------------------|-------------------------------|---|
| **Online return (50 ep)** | 16.80 ± 3.49 | **16.12 ± 4.24** | -0.68 |
| Held-out real NLL | 1.9027 | 1.9054 | +0.003 |
| Held-out real acc | 44.75% | 44.69% | -0.06% |
| Held-out Δ(z-r) acc | -6.62% | **-7.26%** | larger content gap |
| Golden real acc | 79.61% | **93.81%** | +14.20% (stronger memorization) |
| Golden Δ(z-r) acc | -59.57% | -79.06% | — |

### Verdict

- **4× more training does not raise return.** 16.12 vs 16.80 is within noise — both are near the AWR baseline of 16.30.
- **The policy used the embedding *more strongly* under longer training** (held-out Δ(z-r) acc grew from -6.62% to -7.26%), and BC's golden memorization went from 79.61% → 93.81% — yet none of that extra "use" translated into online return gains.
- Taken together with Experiment 4 (adversarial prompts also don't change return): the `freeze_obs_bcawr` architecture has an online-return ceiling near 16.x regardless of how much the imagination pathway is exercised, either in training volume or content variance. The pathway provides a regularizer-like signal, not a planning input.

## Experiment 6: bc_obs_stopgrad — From-Scratch BC+AWR with BC Obs-Gradient Detached

Separates "freeze_obs_bcawr is good because AWR pretraining warmed up obs" from "good because BC can't corrupt obs gradient". From-scratch ActorCriticAugLN (no pretrained checkpoint), 100K steps, AWR updates obs branch normally but BC's gradient is detached at the obs-branch output (`ao`/`co`).

| Checkpoint | BC obs-grad | AWR pretrained | Return | vs AWR 16.30 |
|------------|:-----------:|:--------------:|--------|--------------|
| awr_aug_debug (pure AWR) | — | — | 16.30 ± 3.61 | — |
| awr_bc_aug_debug (unrestricted BC+AWR) | ✓ | ✗ | 7.46 ± 4.91 | **-8.84** |
| **bc_obs_stopgrad** | ✗ (detached) | ✗ | **12.98 ± 4.77** | -3.32 |
| freeze_obs_bcawr | ✗ (frozen) | ✓ | 16.80 ± 3.49 | +0.50 |

### Validation (bc_obs_stopgrad)

| Split | real NLL | zero NLL | shuf NLL | real acc | zero acc | shuf acc | Δ(z-r) acc |
|-------|----------|----------|----------|----------|----------|----------|-------------|
| Held-out | 1.9933 | 2.6373 | 3.0668 | 42.86% | 35.95% | 30.55% | -6.91% |
| Golden   | 0.5188 | 4.6296 | 1.2706 | 80.83% | 11.20% | 61.72% | -69.63% |

### Verdict

- **Blocking BC's obs-pathway gradient alone recovers ~5.5 points** of BC toxicity (7.46 → 12.98), but **still falls 3.3 points short of AWR baseline**. Both mechanisms contribute:
  - BC obs-pathway gradient accounts for **~5.5 points** of the 8.84-point BC collapse.
  - AWR pretraining's warm-started obs weights account for the remaining **~3.8 points** (12.98 → 16.80 when adding frozen obs + pretrained AWR).
- Held-out content gap -6.91% acc is comparable to freeze_obs_bcawr (-6.62%). So "use of the embedding" is similar, but the obs-branch weights matter independently for online return — consistent with the observation that the obs pathway is where task-relevant structure lives.
- Golden real acc 80.83% (vs freeze_obs_bcawr 79.61%): BC memorization of golden is essentially the same; the difference in return is not driven by different memorization levels.

## Experiment 7: Direction-Match Analysis

For each of 50 eval episodes, compare the first policy movement action (LEFT/RIGHT/UP/DOWN) against the first `(dy, dx)` coordinate Gemini mentions in its step-0 narrative. Valid directions: dy<0→UP, dy>0→DOWN, dx<0→LEFT, dx>0→RIGHT. Chance baseline = `|valid_dirs|/4` per episode.

Re-ran 50-ep eval (with per-step action logging) as `*_dirmatch` outputs. `n_scored` = episodes where both a non-origin coord was parsed from Gemini and a movement action occurred.

| Policy | n scored | Match rate | Chance baseline | Lift |
|--------|---------:|-----------:|----------------:|-----:|
| awr_aug_debug (pure AWR) | 46/50 | 47.8% | 33.7% | **+14.1pp** |
| freeze_obs_bcawr          | 47/50 | 68.1% | 33.5% | **+34.6pp** |
| awr_bc_aug_debug (toxic BC+AWR) | 48/50 | 62.5% | 35.9% | +26.6pp |

### Verdict

- **All three policies move with Gemini's predicted destination significantly more often than chance** — this signal is *not* zero. freeze_obs_bcawr is most aligned (+34.6pp), awr_aug_debug least (+14.1pp).
- However, Experiment 4 (adversarial prompts on freeze_obs_bcawr) showed return didn't drop under death-seeking / worst-play prompts. So the high alignment we observe here is **not causal** — replacing Gemini's narrative with harmful content does not reduce return.
- Most parsimonious explanation: **Gemini is predicting what the policy will do given the state**, not steering it. Given a plausible opening state, there are usually one or two "obviously right" first moves; Gemini's state-conditioned forecast and the policy's independent pick tend to coincide, producing the observed alignment without the policy actually following Gemini's plan.
- Caveat: the adversarial prompts may have produced narratives that Gemini still grounded in the state (refusal / soft-refusal), yielding coordinates similar to normal play. Stronger test would replace the narrative with real coordinates pointing away from the policy's actual choice and see if return drops.

Raw numbers in `/data/group_data/rl/geney/eval_results/direction_match_summary.json`.
