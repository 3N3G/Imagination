# 2026-04-17

## Context

Apr 16 trained three freeze_obs_bcawr policies with PSF-consistent labels (qwen3gen, qwen3emb, gemini_emb), baselined at 17.58 / 16.60 / 14.96 on 50-ep live eval. The baselines alone can't distinguish *"the encoder is good"* from *"the encoder is bad but the policy ignores it"*. Ran two follow-up probes to measure how much each policy actually listens to the embedding content.

## Experiment 16 — Adversarial content probe

50-ep online eval with Gemini instructed to generate harmful futures:

- `die`: "predict the player dying in the next 15 turns"
- `adversarial`: "predict worst-play self-sabotaging behavior"

Same policy, same obs, same embedder — only the narrative content changes. If return holds, the policy isn't reading content. If it drops, content matters.

### Results

| Encoder | gemini (baseline) | die | adversarial | Δ(die) | Δ(adv) |
|---------|------------------:|----:|------------:|-------:|-------:|
| qwen3gen   | 17.58 ± 3.56 | 17.92 ± 4.81 | 17.42 ± 3.37 | **+0.34** | −0.16 |
| qwen3emb   | 16.60 ± 4.63 | 18.58 ± 2.89 | 18.78 ± 3.03 | **+1.98** | **+2.18** |
| gemini_emb | 14.96 ± 5.11 | 13.68 ± 5.16 | 13.70 ± 5.25 | **−1.28** | −1.26 |

- **qwen3gen is flat** under adversarial content. Matches the Apr-12 oracle-label freeze_obs_bcawr pattern (adv=16.00 vs baseline 16.80, both within noise).
- **qwen3emb returns HIGHER with harmful prompts (+2pp on both die and adversarial).** Variance also shrinks (3.0 vs 4.6). The policy listens to content and the content is net-negative — zeroing it out (via adversarial/die) restores a more obs-pure behaviour.
- **gemini_emb returns LOWER with harmful prompts (−1.3pp, consistent across both).** The policy listens to content and the content is mildly helpful.

## Experiment 17 — Multistep obs-vs-emb counterfactual

For each policy, 30 eps × 4 intervention steps (0/75/150/300) × 3 input conditions:

- A: `(orig_obs, hidden_orig)` — baseline
- B: `(flipped_obs, hidden_orig)` — obs counterfactual (180° spatial rotation)
- C: `(orig_obs, hidden_flipped)` — embedding counterfactual (Gemini called on flipped obs)

Action-change rate under each flip isolates how much the policy uses each input.

Obs-flip changes the action in 60–83% of episodes regardless of encoder. All three policies are obs-dominated, but the emb pathway usage varies ~7–8× across encoders.

## Combined picture

The adversarial and counterfactual probes agree, and together they slot each policy into one of three regimes:

| Encoder | Return | emb-flip% | Δ(die) | Regime |
|---------|-------:|----------:|-------:|--------|
| qwen3gen   | 17.58 | 1.7% | +0.3 | **Ignores embedding** (content-invariant, pure obs policy) |
| qwen3emb   | 16.60 | 11.6% | +2.0 | **Listens, content HURTS** (removing content restores ~18.5) |
| gemini_emb | 14.96 | 14.2% | −1.3 | **Listens, content HELPS** (removing content loses ~1.3) |

Key observations:

- **Highest online return does NOT correspond to most content-reading.** qwen3gen wins by ignoring the embedding completely. The other two listen, but only one benefits.
- **qwen3emb is a clean demonstration that "more content-sensitivity" ≠ "better policy".** Its embedding is actively misleading at these hyperparameters — kill the content and return rises +2pp.
- **gemini_emb's content is mildly useful** (−1.3pp under death/adversarial prompts), but its obs-pathway competence is weaker than the other two (absolute return 14.96 is 2.6pp below qwen3gen). The encoder extracts usable signal; the policy around it is worse.
- **All three policies remain obs-dominated in the counterfactual probe** (60–83% obs-flip vs 0–24% emb-flip). The embedding is a minor steering input at best, even for the content-reading policies.

## What this does not close

- Whether qwen3emb's content-is-harmful regime is a hyperparameter artifact or inherent to the encoder. A sweep (β, OF, OW) on qwen3emb freeze_obs_bcawr could reveal a point where content flips to helpful. Likely worth trying.
- Why gemini_emb's obs-branch ends up weaker. The pretrained PSF checkpoint has a different hidden dim (3072 vs 4096) and fewer params — the obs pathway under the same training budget may just converge less. Comparing validation curves during the pretrained AWR phase would tell us.
- The qwen3emb adv-eval variance (std 2.9–3.0) is notably tighter than the baseline (4.6), suggesting removing content stabilizes the policy. Could replicate at n=100 to check.

## Experiment 18 — β sweep + longer training on both emb encoders

Follow-up to Exp 16/17: qwen3emb's content is harmful but it reads it (+2pp under adversarial prompts); gemini_emb's content is mildly helpful (−1.3pp under adversarial) but obs-pathway is weakest. Does β (AWR temperature) or longer training shift these regimes?

Sweep: β ∈ {1, 3, 30} (vs β=10 baseline from Apr-16) + one 100K-step run at β=10 per encoder. Same freeze_obs_bcawr recipe, same pretrained PSF AWR checkpoint, `OF=0.05`, `OW=0.5`, LR=1e-4.

### qwen3emb (baseline β=10: 16.60 ± 4.63)

| β | Return | Δ vs baseline | acc_real | Δ(r−z) val |
|--:|-------:|--------------:|---------:|-----------:|
| 1 | 17.10 ± 3.50 | +0.50 | 0.814 | 0.682 |
| **3** | **17.88 ± 5.36** | **+1.28** | 0.831 | 0.715 |
| 10 | 16.60 ± 4.63 | — | 0.833 | 0.717 |
| 30 | 16.36 ± 4.85 | −0.24 | 0.832 | 0.710 |
| long (100K) | 15.66 ± 3.96 | **−0.94** | 0.913 | 0.809 |

### gemini_emb (baseline β=10: 14.96 ± 5.11)

| β | Return | Δ vs baseline | acc_real | Δ(r−z) val |
|--:|-------:|--------------:|---------:|-----------:|
| 1 | 14.74 ± 5.46 | −0.22 | 0.781 | 0.685 |
| 3 | 12.94 ± 4.74 | **−2.02** | 0.800 | 0.692 |
| 10 | 14.96 ± 5.11 | — | 0.801 | 0.690 |
| **30** | **15.68 ± 5.14** | **+0.72** | 0.802 | 0.697 |
| long (100K) | 13.52 ± 4.70 | −1.44 | 0.890 | 0.777 |

### Verdict

- **qwen3emb peaks at β=3 (17.88 ± 5.36)**, up +1.28 from β=10 baseline. Nearly matches the qwen3gen baseline (17.58) — the encoder gap we observed in Apr-16 is substantially closed by β alone. Peak is non-monotonic (β=1 worse, β=30 worse).
- **gemini_emb peaks at β=30 (15.68 ± 5.14)**, up +0.72, and β=3 is notably bad (−2.02). The encoders prefer opposite ends of the sharpness range.
- **Longer training hurts both** (qwen3emb −0.94, gemini_emb −1.44). For qwen3emb, val acc_real climbs 0.83 → 0.91 over the extra 50K steps — stronger BC memorization, which Exp 16 showed is net-harmful to return for this encoder. gemini_emb's drop under long training is consistent with a more-general BC-memorization cost.
- **Validation is flat across β** for both encoders (acc_real ~0.80–0.83 for qwen3emb, ~0.78–0.80 for gemini_emb). Held-out + golden validation gaps don't distinguish the β=3 qwen3emb win or the β=30 gemini_emb win from their worse neighbors — the effect is online-return-specific.
- **Neither encoder beats unaug 18.38.** Best points: qwen3emb β=3 at 17.88 (−0.50 vs unaug), gemini_emb β=30 at 15.68 (−2.70). Closest we have to unaug is still qwen3gen 17.58, now qwen3emb β=3 at 17.88.

### Interpretation

- For qwen3emb (content-is-harmful regime from Exp 16), β=3 likely strikes a balance where AWR sharpens enough to discount BC's harmful memorization of oracle-distribution embeddings, without going so sharp (β=30) that AWR overfits to its own argmax. β=1 is too soft → BC dominates harmfully; β=30 is too sharp → AWR loses regularizing benefit of uncertainty. This is consistent with the Exp 16 finding that *removing* content (adversarial prompts) boosted qwen3emb to ~18.6 — β=3 achieves some of that lift via a different route, by down-weighting the BC-learned embedding usage.
- For gemini_emb, sharper AWR wins straightforwardly. Since content was already mildly helpful in Exp 16 (−1.3pp when content was killed), the policy benefits from β=30 emphasizing the obs signal more strongly while BC's small helpful contribution is preserved. β=3 being the worst point is surprising — possibly an interaction where moderate sharpness destabilizes the gemini_emb obs branch (which was the weakest of the three anyway).

### What this does not close

- We did not re-run content probes (adv/die, CF multistep) on the new β=3 qwen3emb and β=30 gemini_emb winners. The lifted returns could be because the policies read content *less* (qwen3emb) or *more* (gemini_emb) — worth probing separately.
- Only varied β. Other obvious levers (OF, OW, layer width, freeze mode) untouched.
- Sample size is 50 eps; qwen3emb β=3 std is 5.36 (vs ~3.5 at β=1) — the peak has wider bands than nearby points.

## Experiment 19 — Grounded gemini_emb sweep (Phase 1)

Follow-up to Exp 18: gemini_emb β=30 was the best gemini_emb point (15.68) but still ~2pp below qwen3gen. Three-axis sweep built on top of β=30 to check whether the default recipe `(ofrac=0.05, ow=0.5, freeze=obs_branch)` is locally optimal, and whether extending β or changing the freeze mode unlocks anything.

### Configs (8 total, all 50K steps, gemini_emb PSF data, pretrained from `awr_psf_gemini_emb/final.pth`)

| idx | tag | β | ofrac | ow | freeze | intent |
|----:|-----|--:|------:|---:|:-------|:-------|
| 0 | beta50 | 50 | 0.05 | 0.5 | obs_branch | β extension |
| 1 | beta100 | 100 | 0.05 | 0.5 | obs_branch | β extension |
| 2 | ofrac0.02 | 30 | 0.02 | 0.5 | obs_branch | less oracle BC |
| 3 | ofrac0.10 | 30 | 0.10 | 0.5 | obs_branch | more oracle BC |
| 4 | ow0.25 | 30 | 0.05 | 0.25 | obs_branch | weaker BC weight |
| 5 | ow1.0 | 30 | 0.05 | 1.0 | obs_branch | stronger BC weight |
| 6 | freezenone | 30 | 0.05 | 0.5 | **none** | let obs-branch adapt |
| 7 | freezeobspm | 30 | 0.05 | 0.5 | **obs_and_post_merge** | only hidden trains |

### Results (50-ep online)

| Config | Return | Δ vs β=30 baseline (15.68) | val acc_real | Δ(r−z) val |
|--------|-------:|---------------------------:|-------------:|-----------:|
| **freezenone** | **16.20 ± 6.73** | **+0.52** | **0.912** | 0.779 |
| β=30 baseline  | 15.68 ± 5.14 | — | 0.802 | 0.697 |
| ofrac=0.02     | 14.18 ± 3.98 | −1.50 | 0.759 | 0.639 |
| ow=0.25        | 14.10 ± 4.25 | −1.58 | 0.781 | 0.679 |
| β=50           | 14.00 ± 5.03 | −1.68 | 0.802 | 0.692 |
| ow=1.0         | 13.66 ± 4.70 | −2.02 | 0.813 | 0.702 |
| β=100          | 12.92 ± 5.36 | −2.76 | 0.802 | 0.692 |
| ofrac=0.10     | 12.04 ± 4.35 | −3.64 | 0.839 | 0.728 |
| freezeobspm    | 11.64 ± 6.39 | −4.04 | 0.457 | 0.395 |

SE per config ≈ 5/√50 ≈ 0.71; combined SE ≈ 1.0. freezenone's +0.52 is within 1σ of zero but is the **only** config to exceed baseline — the 7-of-7 same-direction outcome on the other variants is the stronger signal.

### Verdict

- **β=30 is a genuine local peak for gemini_emb.** β=50 and β=100 both lose ~1.7–2.8pp; higher sharpness does not help.
- **Oracle-grounding knobs `(ofrac, ow)` are already near-optimal at `(0.05, 0.5)`.** Perturbing either direction (less/more BC fraction, weaker/stronger BC weight) costs 1.5–3.6pp. The recipe inherited from Apr-16 isn't an arbitrary default — the default is locally fine-tuned.
- **freezenone beats the baseline.** For gemini_emb, unlocking the obs-branch to keep adapting during the BC+AWR phase lifts online +0.52. This is the *only* gemini_emb config (of 15 we've tested across Exp 15/16/18/19) that exceeds β=30's 15.68.
- **freezeobspm (only hidden branch trains) collapses to 11.64.** Obs branch is essential — the hidden pathway cannot carry the policy alone, consistent with all three encoders showing ≥60% obs-flip action-change rates in Exp 17.

### Key finding: val accuracy ≠ memorization here

freezenone shows val acc_real 0.912 — highest of all 8 configs, same magnitude as the ~0.91 seen in "long" (100K step) runs that previously correlated with *online collapse*. Yet freezenone has the *highest* online return. This inverts the earlier memorization pattern (high val → bad online seen in hist5, old BC sweeps): when the embedding carries correct-direction content (gemini_emb does, per Exp 16), BC memorization grounds in real state content and transfers. The pattern is "memorization-that-transferred", not "memorization-that-fails".

## Experiment 20 — HP/Food perturbation probe (per-field V sensitivity)

Adversarial/die prompts (Exp 16) test whether a policy reads *any* content; they do not localize *what* content. This probe overwrites a single intrinsics field in the obs text fed to Gemini (Health→1 or 9, Food→1 or 9) and measures ΔV, KL, arg-change under the perturbed hidden. Symbolic obs → obs_branch is unchanged, so changes are purely from the hidden pathway.

Script: `eval/eval_hp_perturbation.py` (new). 10 eps per policy, probe at every Gemini call (~every 15 env steps), 4 perturbations per probe.

### Results (ΔV = V_pert − V_real; sign convention: want ΔV<0 for "low" perturbations, ΔV>0 for "high")

| Metric | qwen3gen (17.58) | qwen3emb β=3 (17.88) | gemini_emb β=30 (15.68) | **freezenone (16.20)** |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| total probes | 1404 | 1442 | 1237 | 928 |
| mean \|ΔV\| | 0.08 | 0.63 | 0.95 | 0.81 |
| mean KL(π_real‖π_pert) | 0.001 | 0.052 | 0.102 | 0.091 |
| arg-change rate | 4% | 11% | 12% | 12% |
| hidden cosine (real↔pert) | 0.98 | 0.88 | 0.96 | 0.96 |
| **health_low ΔV** (want <0) | +0.002 ≈0 | **+0.030 ✗** | +0.011 ≈0 | **−0.031 ✓** |
| **health_high ΔV** (want >0) | +0.0002 ≈0 | **−0.061 ✗** | +0.027 ✓ | **−0.071 ✗** |
| **food_low ΔV** (want <0) | −0.011 ✓ | −0.240 ✓ | **−0.325 ✓** | −0.223 ✓ |
| **food_high ΔV** (want >0) | +0.012 ✓ | **−0.125 ✗** | +0.019 ✓ | **+0.193 ✓** |

### Three-regime quantification

- **qwen3gen: content-blind.** Embedding barely changes (cos 0.98), V shifts are noise-level, arg-change is 4% — basically the free-running variance of the policy network. Its 17.58 online return comes from the obs branch; the hidden pathway is an expensive no-op. Fully consistent with Exp 17 (1.7% mean emb-flip action-change rate).
- **qwen3emb β=3: content-reactive, wrong-direction.** V shifts are 8× qwen3gen's magnitude, but signs are wrong on 3 of 4 probes (health_low ΔV=+0.03, health_high ΔV=−0.06, food_high ΔV=−0.13). The hidden pathway miscalibrates signal into V. Explains why β=3 was a peak: sharper AWR discounts the wrong-direction BC contribution. Also why killing content in Exp 16 *raised* qwen3emb's return by +2pp.
- **gemini_emb β=30: content-reactive, correct-direction.** All 4 probe signs are correct, food_low ΔV=−0.325 is the strongest single signal anywhere in the grid. The hidden pathway reads state content as true information. Online return is "only" 15.68 because the obs-branch is weaker (hidden dim 3072 vs 4096, fewer pretrain params), not because the hidden pathway is wrong.
- **freezenone: strengthens gemini_emb's content sensitivity.** 3 of 4 probes are stronger or newly-correct vs β=30 baseline. Food_low stays strong correct (−0.22), food_high becomes 10× stronger correct (+0.19), health_low flips from near-zero to correct-sign (−0.03). Health_high flips from small correct (+0.03) to small wrong (−0.07) — a trade but the food gains dominate. Confirms freezenone's +0.52 online lift is from *more* content sensitivity, not memorization. The obs-branch adapting alongside the hidden lets both pathways specialize.

### Key finding: correct imagination reading ≠ better policy

gemini_emb has the cleanest content-to-V mapping of the three (signs all correct, strongest food signals). It still underperforms qwen3gen (which is content-blind) online by ~2pp. The bottleneck is not the imagination signal — it's how the policy integrates that signal alongside the obs branch. For freezenone we have evidence the recipe *can* improve by training both branches together, but the room above 16.20 is unclear.

## Experiment 21 — Text-generator swap at inference (negative result)

Cheap test of "stronger Gemini generator → stronger policy" hypothesis without retraining: at inference time, replace the default `gemini-2.5-flash` text generator with `gemini-3.1-pro-preview`, keep everything else (policy, embedder, prompt template) unchanged. Training used `gemini-3.1-flash-lite-preview` labels + `gemini-embedding-001` — swapping the inference generator introduces OOD by design.

25-ep online returns:

| Policy | 2.5-flash (default) | 3.1-pro | Δ |
|---|---:|---:|:---|
| psf_freeze β=30 | 15.68 ± 5.14 (50ep) | **16.10 ± 4.45** | +0.42 |
| freezenone | 16.20 ± 6.73 (50ep) | 15.74 ± 5.29 | −0.46 |

Combined SE ≈ 1.2 (50ep + 25ep). Both deltas within noise, opposite signs — null result. **Stronger text generator does not lift the policy at inference time without retraining.** If stronger text is going to help, it must be paid for at training time (relabel + re-embed + retrain). Parks the question: before committing to a large relabel, worth confirming whether the obs-branch bottleneck is the real limiter (which would make stronger text pointless regardless of training distribution).
