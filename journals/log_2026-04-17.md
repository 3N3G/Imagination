# 2026-04-17

## Context

Apr 16 trained three freeze_obs_bcawr policies with PSF-consistent labels (qwen3gen, qwen3emb, gemini_emb), baselined at 17.58 / 16.60 / 14.96 on 50-ep live eval. The baselines alone can't distinguish *"the encoder is good"* from *"the encoder is bad but the policy ignores it"*. Ran two follow-up probes to measure how much each policy actually listens to the embedding content.

## Experiment 16 — Adversarial content probe

50-ep online eval with Gemini instructed to generate harmful futures:

- `die`: "predict the player dying in the next 15 turns"
- `adversarial`: "predict worst-play self-sabotaging behavior"

Same policy, same obs, same embedder — only the narrative content changes. If return holds, the policy isn't reading content. If it drops, content matters.

### Results

| Encoder | gemini (baseline) | die | adversarial | Δ(die) | Δ(adv) |
|---------|------------------:|----:|------------:|-------:|-------:|
| qwen3gen   | 17.58 ± 3.56 | 17.92 ± 4.81 | 17.42 ± 3.37 | **+0.34** | −0.16 |
| qwen3emb   | 16.60 ± 4.63 | 18.58 ± 2.89 | 18.78 ± 3.03 | **+1.98** | **+2.18** |
| gemini_emb | 14.96 ± 5.11 | 13.68 ± 5.16 | 13.70 ± 5.25 | **−1.28** | −1.26 |

- **qwen3gen is flat** under adversarial content. Matches the Apr-12 oracle-label freeze_obs_bcawr pattern (adv=16.00 vs baseline 16.80, both within noise).
- **qwen3emb returns HIGHER with harmful prompts (+2pp on both die and adversarial).** Variance also shrinks (3.0 vs 4.6). The policy listens to content and the content is net-negative — zeroing it out (via adversarial/die) restores a more obs-pure behaviour.
- **gemini_emb returns LOWER with harmful prompts (−1.3pp, consistent across both).** The policy listens to content and the content is mildly helpful.

## Experiment 17 — Multistep obs-vs-emb counterfactual

For each policy, 30 eps × 4 intervention steps (0/75/150/300) × 3 input conditions:

- A: `(orig_obs, hidden_orig)` — baseline
- B: `(flipped_obs, hidden_orig)` — obs counterfactual (180° spatial rotation)
- C: `(orig_obs, hidden_flipped)` — embedding counterfactual (Gemini called on flipped obs)

Action-change rate under each flip isolates how much the policy uses each input.

Obs-flip changes the action in 60–83% of episodes regardless of encoder. All three policies are obs-dominated, but the emb pathway usage varies ~7–8× across encoders.

## Combined picture

The adversarial and counterfactual probes agree, and together they slot each policy into one of three regimes:

| Encoder | Return | emb-flip% | Δ(die) | Regime |
|---------|-------:|----------:|-------:|--------|
| qwen3gen   | 17.58 | 1.7% | +0.3 | **Ignores embedding** (content-invariant, pure obs policy) |
| qwen3emb   | 16.60 | 11.6% | +2.0 | **Listens, content HURTS** (removing content restores ~18.5) |
| gemini_emb | 14.96 | 14.2% | −1.3 | **Listens, content HELPS** (removing content loses ~1.3) |

Key observations:

- **Highest online return does NOT correspond to most content-reading.** qwen3gen wins by ignoring the embedding completely. The other two listen, but only one benefits.
- **qwen3emb is a clean demonstration that "more content-sensitivity" ≠ "better policy".** Its embedding is actively misleading at these hyperparameters — kill the content and return rises +2pp.
- **gemini_emb's content is mildly useful** (−1.3pp under death/adversarial prompts), but its obs-pathway competence is weaker than the other two (absolute return 14.96 is 2.6pp below qwen3gen). The encoder extracts usable signal; the policy around it is worse.
- **All three policies remain obs-dominated in the counterfactual probe** (60–83% obs-flip vs 0–24% emb-flip). The embedding is a minor steering input at best, even for the content-reading policies.

## What this does not close

- Whether qwen3emb's content-is-harmful regime is a hyperparameter artifact or inherent to the encoder. A sweep (β, OF, OW) on qwen3emb freeze_obs_bcawr could reveal a point where content flips to helpful. Likely worth trying.
- Why gemini_emb's obs-branch ends up weaker. The pretrained PSF checkpoint has a different hidden dim (3072 vs 4096) and fewer params — the obs pathway under the same training budget may just converge less. Comparing validation curves during the pretrained AWR phase would tell us.
- The qwen3emb adv-eval variance (std 2.9–3.0) is notably tighter than the baseline (4.6), suggesting removing content stabilizes the policy. Could replicate at n=100 to check.

## Experiment 18 — β sweep + longer training on both emb encoders

Follow-up to Exp 16/17: qwen3emb's content is harmful but it reads it (+2pp under adversarial prompts); gemini_emb's content is mildly helpful (−1.3pp under adversarial) but obs-pathway is weakest. Does β (AWR temperature) or longer training shift these regimes?

Sweep: β ∈ {1, 3, 30} (vs β=10 baseline from Apr-16) + one 100K-step run at β=10 per encoder. Same freeze_obs_bcawr recipe, same pretrained PSF AWR checkpoint, `OF=0.05`, `OW=0.5`, LR=1e-4.

### qwen3emb (baseline β=10: 16.60 ± 4.63)

| β | Return | Δ vs baseline | acc_real | Δ(r−z) val |
|--:|-------:|--------------:|---------:|-----------:|
| 1 | 17.10 ± 3.50 | +0.50 | 0.814 | 0.682 |
| **3** | **17.88 ± 5.36** | **+1.28** | 0.831 | 0.715 |
| 10 | 16.60 ± 4.63 | — | 0.833 | 0.717 |
| 30 | 16.36 ± 4.85 | −0.24 | 0.832 | 0.710 |
| long (100K) | 15.66 ± 3.96 | **−0.94** | 0.913 | 0.809 |

### gemini_emb (baseline β=10: 14.96 ± 5.11)

| β | Return | Δ vs baseline | acc_real | Δ(r−z) val |
|--:|-------:|--------------:|---------:|-----------:|
| 1 | 14.74 ± 5.46 | −0.22 | 0.781 | 0.685 |

| long (100K) | 13.52 ± 4.70 | −1.44 | 0.890 | 0.777 |

### Verdict

- **qwen3emb peaks at β=3 (17.88 ± 5.36)**, up +1.28 from β=10 baseline. Nearly matches the qwen3gen baseline (17.58) — the encoder gap we observed in Apr-16 is substantially closed by β alone. Peak is non-monotonic (β=1 worse, β=30 worse).
- **gemini_emb peaks at β=30 (15.68 ± 5.14)**, up +0.72, and β=3 is notably bad (−2.02). The encoders prefer opposite ends of the sharpness range.
- **Longer training hurts both** (qwen3emb −0.94, gemini_emb −1.44). For qwen3emb, val acc_real climbs 0.83 → 0.91 over the extra 50K steps — stronger BC memorization, which Exp 16 showed is net-harmful to return for this encoder. gemini_emb's drop under long training is consistent with a more-general BC-memorization cost.
- **Validation is flat across β** for both encoders (acc_real ~0.80–0.83 for qwen3emb, ~0.78–0.80 for gemini_emb). Held-out + golden validation gaps don't distinguish the β=3 qwen3emb win or the β=30 gemini_emb win from their worse neighbors — the effect is online-return-specific.
- **Neither encoder beats unaug 18.38.** Best points: qwen3emb β=3 at 17.88 (−0.50 vs unaug), gemini_emb β=30 at 15.68 (−2.70). Closest we have to unaug is still qwen3gen 17.58, now qwen3emb β=3 at 17.88.

### Interpretation

- For qwen3emb (content-is-harmful regime from Exp 16), β=3 likely strikes a balance where AWR sharpens enough to discount BC's harmful memorization of oracle-distribution embeddings, without going so sharp (β=30) that AWR overfits to its own argmax. β=1 is too soft → BC dominates harmfully; β=30 is too sharp → AWR loses regularizing benefit of uncertainty. This is consistent with the Exp 16 finding that *removing* content (adversarial prompts) boosted qwen3emb to ~18.6 — β=3 achieves some of that lift via a different route, by down-weighting the BC-learned embedding usage.
- For gemini_emb, sharper AWR wins straightforwardly. Since content was already mildly helpful in Exp 16 (−1.3pp when content was killed), the policy benefits from β=30 emphasizing the obs signal more strongly while BC's small helpful contribution is preserved. β=3 being the worst point is surprising — possibly an interaction where moderate sharpness destabilizes the gemini_emb obs branch (which was the weakest of the three anyway).

### What this does not close

- We did not re-run content probes (adv/die, CF multistep) on the new β=3 qwen3emb and β=30 gemini_emb winners. The lifted returns could be because the policies read content *less* (qwen3emb) or *more* (gemini_emb) — worth probing separately.
- Only varied β. Other obvious levers (OF, OW, layer width, freeze mode) untouched.
- Sample size is 50 eps; qwen3emb β=3 std is 5.36 (vs ~3.5 at β=1) — the peak has wider bands than nearby points.
