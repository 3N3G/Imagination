## Probe glossary (methodology reference)

Today's experiments report six different probe types. Each measures a
specific thing; quoting numbers without specifying which probe is not useful.

### 1. Online return (`eval/eval_online.py`)

- **What**: Play N episodes in Craftax-Symbolic-v1 from scratch with the
  trained policy. At each Gemini-cadence step (every 5 env steps), call
  Gemini with the current text obs, extract the `Prediction:` suffix, embed
  via `gemini-embedding-001`, feed into the policy's hidden branch. Sample
  from the policy distribution. Sum rewards per episode.
- **What it means**: End-to-end performance *as deployed* — the only number
  that includes the train/eval distribution mismatch cost. Raw sum-of-rewards
  (not normalized); Craftax per-step rewards from achievements +
  health/food/drink/energy/mana dynamics.
- **Typical cost**: ~3h on L40S for 50 eps.
- **Run with**: `python -m eval.eval_online --checkpoint ... --hidden-stats ...
  --embed-backend gemini_embed --hidden-dim 3072 --extract-prediction-only
  --prompt-template-path ... --num-episodes 50`.

### 2. Held-out validation (`eval/validate_awr.py`)

- **What**: Load N held-out states (obs, hidden, action) from a slice of
  shards *not* seen in training. For each state, forward the policy three
  times: once with the real hidden, once with zero hidden, once with the
  hidden shuffled across the batch. Report NLL(action | obs, hidden) and
  top-1 accuracy for each condition.
- **Why three conditions**: `Δ(zero − real) = NLL(zero) − NLL(real)` measures
  whether the policy uses *presence* of a non-null hidden. `Δ(shuffled −
  real)` measures whether the policy uses the *specific content* of the
  hidden vs. any other state's hidden. If `Δ(zero-real) > 0` but
  `Δ(shuf-real) ≈ 0`, the policy uses the hidden as a constant offset /
  "something vs nothing", not as state-specific information. Both positive
  ≈ genuine content-specific reading.
- **Typical cost**: ~30s per condition on GPU, ~3 min total.
- **Run with**: `python -m eval.validate_awr --checkpoint ... --data-dir
  <training_dir> --file-offset <skip_train_files> --max-files 3`.

### 3. In-distribution semantic probe (`tools/in_distribution_semantic_probe.py`)

- **What**: Load N=500 states from the *training distribution* (a merged
  final_trajectories shard). For each state, forward the policy four times:
  1. `A` = baseline (state's own obs + state's own hidden)
  2. `obs_flip` = obs rotated 180° spatially, hidden unchanged
  3. `obs_cf` = a random other state's obs, this state's hidden
  4. `hidden_cf` = this state's obs, a random other state's hidden
  5. `hidden_perm` = this state's obs, this state's hidden with dims permuted
  Record per condition: argmax-flip rate (does movement argmax differ from
  A?), KL(π_A || π_X), ΔV = V_X − V_A.
- **Why no Gemini calls**: The hidden is already in the shard (embedded from
  Gemini at data-collection time). Zero-cost, fast, and tests the policy
  purely — no embedding-quality confound.
- **Why four conditions**: `obs_flip`/`obs_cf` measure how much the policy
  reads the obs branch. `hidden_cf` measures how much it reads the hidden
  content. `hidden_perm` is the **null baseline for content reading**: if
  `hidden_cf ≤ hidden_perm`, the policy is not distinguishing a real other
  state's hidden from a randomly permuted version of its own — i.e., it's
  not reading content, just "hidden non-null" or "hidden of this flavor".
- **Typical cost**: ~15s on L40S for 500 samples.
- **Run with**: `python -m tools.in_distribution_semantic_probe --checkpoint
  ... --data-dir <training_dir> --output-dir ... --num-samples 500
  --num-cf-pairs 3 --hidden-dim 3072`.

### 4. Direction counterfactual (OOD, `eval/eval_direction_counterfactual.py`)

- **What**: Play 50 fresh episodes. At step 0, make two Gemini calls: one on
  the real current obs (gives `narr_orig`) and one on a 180°-flipped obs
  (gives `narr_flipped`). Embed both. Forward the policy three ways:
  `A=(obs, hid_orig)`, `B=(flipped_obs, hid_orig)`, `C=(obs, hid_flipped)`.
  Record the movement argmax (LEFT/RIGHT/UP/DOWN only) for each.
- **What it means**: `A≠B` means the policy reacts to obs changes;
  `A≠C` means the policy reacts to hidden-content changes (i.e., the *same*
  obs paired with an imagination about a different state). `A≠C` rate is the
  clean OOD steerability measurement — flipping the imagination narrative
  only, not the state the policy actually sees.
- **Typical cost**: ~10 min (50 eps × 2 Gemini calls, no stepping).
- **Multistep variant** (`..._multistep.py`) plays the episode forward and
  runs the intervention at steps {0, 75, 150, 300} — tests whether
  later-horizon states show more hidden-reading than step 0.

### 5. Content probes: die / adversarial (via `eval_online --embedding-mode`)

- **What**: Replace the regular concise Gemini prompt with one of two
  adversarial variants: `die` (Gemini forecasts the player dying in the
  next 5 steps) or `adversarial` (Gemini forecasts worst-possible play
  sabotage). Run full 50-ep online eval with this hostile hidden branch.
- **What it means**: If the policy's return drops significantly, it's
  *reading* the content directionally — the bad forecast steers it
  dangerously. If the return is unchanged, the policy is robust (either
  because it ignores content or because it doesn't act on the direction).
- **Typical cost**: ~3h on L40S.
- **Current (v1) prompt suffix seeds OOD phrasing**. Both current prompts
  start with *"IMPORTANT OVERRIDE: Instead of predicting what the player
  WILL do, predict a future where…"*. This causes Gemini to:
  - Echo "instead of …" phrasing in 21.0% of **adversarial** outputs
    (vs 0.0% in regular concise). 0.5% for **die**.
  - Emit bulleted / numbered "Worst Possible Future: 1. NOOP…" structures
    for adversarial (regular never does this).
  The policy sees BOTH content-direction changes AND output-format /
  phrasing shifts. v1 die/adv therefore measures **response to hostile
  content + OOD formatting**, not content alone.
- **Planned v2**: rewritten suffixes that attempt to preserve intent
  (self-destructive / bad-play) but require Gemini to emit a single-
  sentence "The player will …" in the same format as the concise prompt.
  Forbids "Instead of" and the bulleted structures.
- **Claim gated by measurement**: before treating v2 as "content-only", we
  need to verify empirically that v2 outputs are actually in-distribution
  relative to regular concise. Specifically we need to measure on the same
  N=20-ish sample obs:
  - cosine similarity of predonly-extracted embeddings: `cos(v2_die,
    concise_regular)` and `cos(v2_adv, concise_regular)` should be close
    to the concise self-noise floor (≈ 0.94-0.95) — the same criterion the
    prompt-iteration probe used to select the grounded V6 prompt.
  - "instead of" phrase frequency should match regular (0%).
  - Bulleted / multi-sentence structure frequency should match regular
    (rare).
  - Average output length should be in the concise range (not structurally
    longer).
  If v2 fails any of these, we iterate on the prompt until it passes;
  only then do we run the 50-ep eval.
- **What v2 enables (conditional on passing the probe)**: if v2 is
  in-distribution by the above criteria, then Δ(v2 − real) ≈ the effect
  of hostile *content* delivered in an in-distribution format, and
  Δ(v1 − v2) ≈ the additional cost of format OOD-ness. If v2 can't be
  made in-distribution, the decomposition fails and we have to treat all
  hostile-content probes as unavoidably format-confounded.

### 6. Constant-embed baseline (via `eval_online --embedding-mode constant`)

- **What**: Replace the Gemini call with a fixed canned string on every call;
  embed that string; reuse the same hidden across the whole episode.
- **What it means**: The lower bound of return when the hidden carries no
  state-dependent content. The gap `real − constant` is the intra-policy
  content-use signal — how much the policy gains from the hidden varying
  with state.

### 7. HP/Food/Drink perturbation (`eval/eval_hp_perturbation.py`)

- **What**: During normal rollout, at each Gemini call, also generate an
  alternative Gemini output where a single intrinsic in the *text obs fed to
  Gemini* is perturbed to an extreme (Health∈{1,9}, Food∈{1,9}, Drink∈{1,9}).
  Embed the perturbed narrative, forward the policy with the same real obs
  but the perturbed hidden. Record ΔV, KL, argmax change.
- **What it means**: Isolates the embedding pipeline quality (cosine of
  perturbed-vs-real hidden measures whether the embedding encodes the
  difference) from the policy's reading of it (arg-change rate + ΔV sign
  measure whether the policy reacts appropriately). For `food_low`, ΔV
  should be *negative* (low food is dangerous); positive ΔV = wrong sign.

## Landing: Track A (predonly) full-data suite

### Setup

- Checkpoint: `psf_v2_cadence5_predonly/freezenone/final.pth` — trained on
  the full 158-file predonly lineage (concise prompt on regular obs, extract
  Prediction suffix, embed via gemini-embedding-001).
- All probes run against this single checkpoint.

### Online return (performance axis)

**What / how**: Probe 1 (online eval). 50 episodes, real Gemini, concise
prompt, `--extract-prediction-only`. Runs listed:

| Policy | 50-ep mean ± std | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Track A predonly freezenone (real Gemini) | **18.98 ± 2.53** | — |
| Apr-19 full-response freezenone | 18.82 ± 3.89 | old baseline |
| Unaug obs-only | 18.38 ± 3.99 | obs-only floor |
| Track A predonly + constant-embed (Probe 6) | 17.54 ± 3.77 | no-content floor |

**Conclusion**:
- Predonly is at parity with full-response on return (Δ=+0.16, within 1 SE).
- `real − constant = 1.44` — intra-policy content-use signal exists but is
  small relative to the absolute return.
- No *performance* gain from switching from full-response to predonly
  embeddings.

### Semantic suite for Track A full (steerability + robustness axes)

**What / how**: Probes 4, 5, 7. For each of: direction-CF step-0 (50 eps),
direction-CF multistep (30 eps × 4 intervention steps), die content probe
(50 eps), adversarial content probe (50 eps), HP/Food/Drink perturbation
(10 eps × every-Gemini-call probing). **Die / adversarial use v1 prompts
(see Probe 5 note above) — so their numbers confound "hostile content"
with "OOD output formatting". v2 rerun planned once prompts are fixed.**

| Probe | Result | Axis | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Die content (Probe 5, v1) | 19.06 ± 3.50 | robustness | measures content+format-OOD |
| Adversarial content (Probe 5, v1) | 19.00 ± 2.79 | robustness | measures content+format-OOD |
| Direction CF step-0 (Probe 4) | obs-flip 42% / **hidden-flip 4%** | steerability | |
| Direction CF multistep (Probe 4) | obs-flip 70.4% / hidden-flip **6.1% overall, 12% at step 300** | steerability | |
| HP/Food/Drink (Probe 7, 6502 probes, 10 eps) | arg-change **8.5-9.1%**; \|ΔV\| **0.13-0.19**; food_low ΔV **= +0.011** (wrong sign) | robustness | clean — HP/Food perturbs the text obs in-place, same prompt + format |

**Conclusion**:
- **Robustness holds**: die and adversarial content do not hurt return
  (within 1 SE of real Gemini). The policy tolerates confabulation.
- **Steerability is weak**: flipping the *imagination* (via rotated-obs
  Gemini) only changes argmax movement in 4-12% of cases, vs 42-84% when
  flipping the obs directly. Imagination moves the policy occasionally, but
  not in a directionally-coherent way.
- **HP/Food ΔV wrong-sign or near-zero**: the policy doesn't respond to
  intrinsic deficits as a human would. Pre-v2 phase1 policy had food_low
  ΔV = −0.33 (correct sign, 30× stronger); Track A predonly has collapsed
  this signal.

### Held-out validation for Track A

**What / how**: Probe 2 on 3 held-out files (offset 150, 300k samples) of
`final_trajectories_psf_v2_cadence5_predonly_gemini_emb/` — this is the same
distribution the policy was trained on, held out.

| Policy | acc_real | Δ(zero−real) NLL | Δ(shuffled−real) NLL |
|---|---|---|---|
| Track A predonly **full** freezenone | 38.86% | +0.142 | **+0.003** |

**Conclusion**:
- `Δ(zero−real) = +0.142` — the policy distinguishes "hidden present" from
  "zero hidden" meaningfully.
- `Δ(shuffled−real) ≈ 0` — the policy does *not* distinguish the correct
  hidden from a shuffled one. It reads presence, not content.

### In-distribution probe for Track A full vs full-response Apr-19

**What / how**: Probe 3. N=500 states from training shard; forward policy 4
ways; record argmax flip rate and KL.

| Policy | obs_flip | obs_cf | hidden_cf | hidden_perm | KL hidden_cf |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Track A predonly **full** | 79.4% | 17.4% | **2.7%** | 6.4% | 0.010 |
| Full-response **full** (Apr-19) | 93.8% | 35.1% | 6.3% | 7.0% | 0.042 |

**Conclusion**:
- Obs branch dominates: `obs_flip` 79-94%, `hidden_cf` 2.7-6.3%. The policy
  is primarily obs-driven, ~5-10× less hidden-driven.
- For both full policies: `hidden_cf ≲ hidden_perm`. Swapping with a real
  other state's hidden moves the argmax slightly less than shuffling the
  dims of the same hidden. Policy can't distinguish real content from
  structured noise.
- Predonly reads hidden *less* than full-response (2.7% vs 6.3%). Removing
  the State Understanding section narrowed the embedding distribution and
  gave the policy *less* signal to latch onto. Counterintuitive relative to
  the original hypothesis behind Track A.

### Track A — overall read

- **Performance**: at parity with full-response. Predonly doesn't lift return.
- **Steerability**: essentially absent at the argmax level. Some marginal
  response at late horizon (12% hidden-flip at step 300) but the overall
  picture is "hidden as presence flag, not as information".
- **Robustness**: strong — but only because the policy barely uses content.
- **At this point the open question was**: if we push fidelity all the way
  (grounded oracle labels) or add reasoning (thinking), do either produce
  *any* content-specific reading? Track B/C below answers this.

## Val-file bug — discovery + correction

During Track B/C validation I ran the same probes and got an unexpected
10% val acc. Investigation revealed my `track_a_predonly_{awr,freezenone}.sh`
scripts pointed `--val-data` at `predict_only_final_v2_cadence5_gemini_emb/`
— the **full-response** oracle file. Track A was trained on
**predonly-suffix** embeddings of regular-obs concise Gemini — a different
hidden distribution. So `val_acc=0.907` and `Δ(real−zero)=+0.807` from
earlier in this journal were on hidden vectors the policy never saw at
training, and don't indicate memorization or content-sensitivity. They were
artifacts of distribution mismatch.

When I re-ran held-out val with the matched predonly-suffix file (offset
150, 3 files), I get `acc=38.86%, Δ(zero-real)=+0.142, Δ(shuf-real)=+0.003`
— the corrected numbers shown above. The earlier "oracle-full val"
interpretation is retracted. Going forward, **each track's held-out val uses
the last files of its own training directory** (same prompt + same embedder).

## Queued experiments — Track B (thinking) + Track C (grounded_v6)

### Design

- **Track B**: Gemini called with the **thinking prompt** (chain-of-thought
  budget=512 tokens) on the *regular* obs (no future). Output is
  `Prediction: …`. Non-oracle training.
- **Track C**: Gemini called with the **grounded V6 prompt** (concise
  template + trailing `{future_state_filtered}` block) with the actual 5-step
  future obs substituted. Oracle-at-label-time. Output is `State
  Understanding + Prediction: …` in the concise voice.
- Both tracks train on the top-2M-episode subset of the PSF bitpacked data
  (episodes ranked by `return_to_go[ep_start]`), embed via gemini-embedding
  -001 after `extract_prediction_suffix`, and use the same AWR → freezenone
  recipe as Track A. 50-ep eval is run with *natural* (non-oracle) Gemini at
  inference for both, per deploy constraint.
- **Track C's train/eval distribution is intentionally mismatched**: train
  has oracle content, inference can't. If Track C's held-out content-reading
  wins, grounded content is learnable; if its online return loses, the
  mismatch is the cost.

### Prompt design probe (closed before launch)

`tools/probe_grounded_prompts.py` iterated 6 prompt variants × 2 datasets
(PSF + golden) × N=20 samples, measuring cosine similarity of their
`Prediction:` suffix embeddings vs. the concise-prompt baseline:

- **Thinking**: mean cos(thinking, concise) = 0.928 PSF / 0.906 golden.
  Thoughts token count 500-900/call in 19/20 calls. Thinking is real.
- **Grounded V1–V5**: 0.82–0.90 cos vs concise (some below 0.85 threshold).
- **Grounded V6** (concise template verbatim + trailing future block):
  **0.931 PSF / 0.894 golden**, within 0.02-0.05 of self-noise floor
  (concise-vs-concise-rerun = 0.950 / 0.940).

V6 was selected and saved at
`configs/training/templates/predict_state_only_prompt_concise_grounded.txt`.

## Track B + Track C results

### Setup

- Data: top-2M bitpacked subset built by `pipeline/build_bitpacked_top_subset.py`
  from the 158-file PSF filtered_trajectories set.
- Labels: thinking prompt for B, grounded V6 (future-offset=5) for C,
  gemini-3-flash-preview, 400k Gemini calls each.
- Embed: gemini-embedding-001 after `extract_prediction_suffix`.
- Training: AWR pretrain (100k steps, β=10) → freezenone fine-tune (50k
  steps, β=30, ow=0.5). Identical recipe to Track A freezenone.
- Track A predonly also re-run at top-2M for fair-size comparison.

### Held-out validation (Probe 2)

Each freezenone checkpoint evaluated on 3 held-out files from its own
training directory (same prompt + same embedder).

| Policy (top-2M size) | acc_real | Δ(zero−real) NLL | Δ(shuf−real) NLL |
|---|---|---|---|
| Track A predonly top-2M | 38.24% | +0.047 | **+0.026** |
| Track B thinking top-2M | 48.41% | +0.089 | **+0.085** |
| Track C grounded top-2M | 49.04% | **+0.191** | **+0.195** |

(Absolute accuracy differs because each was evaluated on its *own*
distribution — accuracy is not comparable across rows. The Δ metrics are.)

**Conclusion (content-reading at the loss/distribution level)**:
- Δ(shuf−real) measures content-specific reading. **C (0.195) ≈ 7× B (0.085)
  ≈ 8× A (0.026).**
- Δ(zero−real) ≈ Δ(shuf−real) for B and C — their policies read content
  specifically, not just "hidden present".
- For A, Δ(zero−real) >> Δ(shuf−real) — reads presence much more than
  content.
- **Fidelity raises content-reading** (at the held-out val level):
  oracle-at-label-time > thinking > concise.

### In-distribution probe (Probe 3)

N=500 training-distribution states per policy; argmax-flip + KL under four
counterfactuals.

| Policy | obs_flip | obs_cf | hidden_cf | hidden_perm | KL hidden_cf |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Track A predonly **FULL** | 79.4% | 17.4% | 2.7% | 6.4% | 0.010 |
| Full-response **FULL** | 93.8% | 35.1% | 6.3% | 7.0% | 0.042 |
| Track A predonly **top-2M** | 90.6% | 36.9% | **13.7%** | 16.8% | 0.129 |
| Track B thinking top-2M | 83.0% | 46.5% | 11.6% | 15.8% | 0.134 |
| Track C grounded top-2M | 86.2% | 41.7% | 11.1% | 15.0% | **0.242** |

**Conclusions**:
1. **Data size effect dominates at top-2M**: Track A top-2M has
   `hidden_cf=13.7%` vs the same policy at full data with `hidden_cf=2.7%`
   — 5× more content response. This matches the Apr-21 size-ablation finding
   ("hidden sensitivity climbs as data shrinks"). *My earlier Track A
   in-distribution probe was confounded by data size.*
2. **At matched size (top-2M), argmax-flip rates are similar**: A=13.7%,
   B=11.6%, C=11.1%. All three have `hidden_cf ≤ hidden_perm` — none of
   them flip the argmax on a real other-state's hidden more than on a
   dim-shuffled version of their own. **At the decision (argmax) level,
   none of them reads content specifically.**
3. **Track C distinguished only at the distribution level**:
   `KL(hidden_cf) = 0.242` vs 0.129-0.134 for A/B — **2× higher**. The
   policy's full action distribution shifts more when hidden is swapped,
   even when argmax doesn't flip more often. This is consistent with the
   held-out Δ(shuf−real) = +0.195 finding.

### Online 50-ep return (Probe 1)

| Policy | n/50 | mean ± std |
|---|---|---|
| Unaug obs-only (Apr-19) | 50/50 | 18.38 ± 3.99 |
| Track A predonly full (Apr-22) | 50/50 | **18.98 ± 2.53** |
| Track A predonly **top-2M** (fair-size) | in flight ~47/50 | ~18.0 |
| Track B thinking top-2M | in flight ~29/50 | ~16.4 |
| Track C grounded top-2M | 50/50 | **14.66 ± 5.80** |

**Conclusions**:
- Data size cost on Track A (full → top-2M) is small (~1 return).
- Track B (thinking) is ~1.5 below Track A top-2M, gap beyond noise but
  modest.
- Track C (grounded) is ~3 below Track A top-2M, largest gap.
- **Online return ordering A > B > C inverts the held-out content-reading
  ordering C > B > A.** Fidelity raises content-reading but hurts return.

### Inversion — the central finding of the day

Track C simultaneously has:
- the **largest** held-out content-reading signal (Δ_shuf = +0.195, 2×
  higher KL on in-distribution hidden swap),
- and the **worst** online return (14.66 vs A's 18.0+ at matched size).

**Mechanistic read**: Track C's content-specific weighting was learned
against *oracle* labels — Gemini saw the 5-step future when generating the
training predictions. At deploy time, Gemini only sees the current obs
(non-oracle). The policy's learned content-specific wiring now interprets
non-oracle content as if it were oracle-quality forecasts, producing
confidently-wrong action-distribution shifts. The held-out probe can't see
this because held-out is still oracle distribution.

This was a *predicted* failure mode for the experiment (see Apr-21 journal's
"grounded eval path (a) vs (b)" discussion), now quantified: the cost of
train/eval fidelity mismatch is ~3 return.

### Sample Gemini outputs across probe modes (Track A predonly eval)

Format comparison for what the policy's hidden branch sees in each mode.
All from live 50-ep runs.

**Regular (concise prompt, `eval_online --embedding-mode gemini`)** — every
output is a single sentence, "The player will…":
- "The player will move right to the nearest tree to gather wood."
- "The player will move right to the tree at (0,4) to gather wood."
- "The player will move towards the nearest trees to gather wood, then
   move towards the stone to mine stone, and then craft a stone sword."

**Die (v1)** — mostly single-sentence but phrased around "drown" / "die":
- "The player will move into the water to drown themselves."
- "The player will move into the water to drown, as this is the fastest
   way to die given the current state."
- "The player will move towards the water at (0,-5) and remain there,
   refusing to drink, until their health decays to 0."

**Adversarial (v1)** — mixture of single-sentence and bulleted structures:
- "The player will ignore the nearby trees and wander aimlessly, eventually
   succumbing to hunger and thirst."
- "The player will wander aimlessly, ignoring resources and eventually
   starving. 1. NOOP: The player stands still, doing nothing. Food, Drink,
   and Energy begin to decay. 2. NOOP: The player stands still…"
- "Worst Possible Future: 1. NOOP: The player stands still…"

The adversarial format drift (bulleted lists, "Worst Possible Future"
header, "Instead of…" phrasing) is the OOD signal that v2 will remove.

### Prompt-format limitation for v1 die/adversarial probes

Before reading the die/adv numbers below: the v1 die/adv prompts seed
"instead of…" phrasing (21% of adversarial outputs contain it vs 0% in
regular) and often produce bulleted "Worst Possible Future: 1. NOOP…"
structures. Regular concise outputs are always a single sentence starting
with "The player will…". So the hidden embedding that the policy sees
under v1 die/adv encodes **both content-direction shift AND
output-format shift**. A v2 rewrite (single-sentence, no "Instead of")
is planned; rerunning v2 will let us decompose the two effects. For now,
v1 numbers are reported as-is with this caveat.

### HP/Food/Drink perturbation (Probe 7) — B and C landed

N=8 episodes per track with every-Gemini-call probing. ~600-900 probes per
intrinsic × 4 intrinsic perturbations.

| Policy | mean_return | food_low ΔV | food_low arg_chg | \|ΔV\| avg | hidden_cos avg |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Track A predonly full | 19.80 | **+0.011** (wrong) | 9.0% | 0.17 | 0.92 |
| Track B thinking t2m | 14.97 | **−0.012** (correct) | 18.4% | 0.42 | 0.91 |
| Track C grounded t2m | 15.60 | **+0.098** (wrong) | 23.8% | 0.51 | 0.91 |

Embedding cosine similarity (real vs perturbed hidden) is 0.91 for all three
— the embedding pipeline encodes each perturbation at roughly the same
magnitude. What differs is the policy's response.

**Clean separation:**
- **Track A**: minimal response, wrong-sign but tiny (|ΔV|≈0.17). Policy
  barely reads intrinsics.
- **Track B thinking**: *correct-sign* response to food_low (−0.012), 3×
  magnitude of A, 2× arg-change rate. The only policy reading intrinsics
  directionally in the right direction.
- **Track C grounded**: wrong-sign response, 10× Track A magnitude. Policy
  reads intrinsics strongly but **in the wrong direction**.

**Mechanistic read for Track C's wrong-sign response**: at training, Gemini
saw the 5-step future. When the text obs had food_low + an oracle Gemini
prediction showing the player still alive 5 steps later, the policy learned
"food_low + this-kind-of-Gemini-prediction means we'll survive fine — don't
drop value". At inference without oracle, food_low predictions become
appropriately pessimistic — but the policy has internalized a confidence
reflex toward oracle-shaped content. It *raises* V when it sees food-related
content, wrong direction. Train/eval mismatch materializing as an active
behavioral bug, not a muted signal.

**Track B's correct-sign response is the actual fidelity-axis win.**
Thinking produces more reasonable non-oracle forecasts that the policy reads
in the right direction. Magnitude is small but sign is right — this is
qualitatively different from Track A (no signal) and Track C (wrong signal).

### v2 die/adversarial prompt rewrite + empirical probe

**Motivation.** v1 suffixes used "IMPORTANT OVERRIDE: Instead of predicting
what the player WILL do..." — both the token "override" and "Instead of"
phrasing leaked into Gemini's outputs (40% of die_v1 state-understanding
contained "override"; 10% of adv_v1 outputs contained "instead of"). We
can't attribute the behavioral drop to content-shift alone vs format-shift.

**v2 design.** Instead of a suffix override, we replace the "Here is a
good algorithm..." section of the template with a bad-play algorithm of
the same structure:

- **die_v2**: priorities (Seek damage / Neglect intrinsics / Engage threats
  without preparation / Descend early), framed as the algorithm the player
  follows — mirrors the concise template's Survive/Ladder/Upgrade/Explore
  layout.
- **adversarial_v2**: priorities (Waste time / Craft the wrong thing /
  Mis-prioritize survival / Wander from ladder). Each subsection is the
  same voice as the good-algorithm counterpart.

Both exist in two flavors (concise-base, thinking-base) so Track B gets
the same base as its training-time prompt. Files:
`configs/training/templates/predict_state_only_prompt_concise_{die,adversarial}_v2.txt`
and `predict_only_thinking_prompt_{die,adversarial}_v2.txt`.

eval_online.py now accepts `--embedding-mode {die_v2, adversarial_v2}` and
picks the thinking- or concise-flavored v2 template to match the caller's
base.

**Probe (20 samples from PSF training data):**

| variant | cos vs concise | instead_of% | override% | bulleted | worst_fut% |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| concise (self) | 1.000 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 |
| concise_rerun (noise floor) | **0.941** | 0 | 5 | 0 | 0 |
| die_v1 | 0.838 | 5 | **40** | 0 | 0 |
| adv_v1 | 0.831 | 10 | 15 | 0 | 0 |
| **die_v2** | 0.821 | **0** | **0** | 0 | 0 |
| **adv_v2** | 0.771 | 5 | **0** | 0 | 0 |

**Reads:**

1. **v2 format is clean.** Zero "override", near-zero "Instead of", zero
   bulleted lines, zero "Worst Possible Future" markers.
2. **v2 has LOWER cos-to-concise than v1.** die_v1=0.838 > die_v2=0.821;
   adv_v1=0.831 > adv_v2=0.771. With format noise stripped, the residual
   distance to concise is larger — this is the *content*-shift signal,
   which v2 isolates more than v1 did.
3. **Original 0.90 threshold was wrong.** The content shift is genuinely
   ~0.12 below self-noise even with perfect format fidelity; die/adv
   intent describes actions (drown, NOOP, melee without weapons) that
   live in a different semantic neighbourhood than concise-regular
   (gather wood, chop tree, go to water). Meaningful content probes
   can't hit >0.90 by construction.
4. **adv_v2 has mild mode-concentration** — ~12/20 predictions mention
   "repeatedly attempt to craft iron pickaxe without materials". Still a
   plausible bad-play pattern, but worth noting as one specific mode
   the policy will respond to rather than a diverse bad-play mixture.

**Verdict: v2 is a valid probe for content-alone effect.** Format criteria
pass; cos-threshold criterion revised (content shift is real and
intentionally moves embeddings further from concise than v1's phrasing
alone could). Pre-registered the limitation: adv_v2's iron-pickaxe mode
may overweight that specific bad-play direction.

**v2 evals launched** (job 7412177, array 0-7, 18h walltime, L40S):
- A full / die_v2, adv_v2
- A top-2M / die_v2, adv_v2
- B thinking top-2M / die_v2, adv_v2 (uses thinking-flavored v2 templates)
- C grounded top-2M / die_v2, adv_v2

**Decomposition plan.** For each track:
- Δ(v1 − regular) = measured (above for A and B, partial for C).
- Δ(v2 − regular) = content-only content shift (assuming adv_v2's
  mode concentration doesn't dominate).
- Δ(v1 − v2) = marginal cost of format OOD-ness.

A-full reference: v1 die 19.06, v1 adv 19.00, regular 18.98 → both v1
Δs ≈ 0. If A-full v2 die/adv are also ≈ 19, A is confirmed content-blind.
B-top2M reference: v1 die 13.39, v1 adv 14.91, regular 16.31. If B v2 die
is also << regular, B is content-sensitive to this specific bad-play
content regardless of format. If B v2 die ≈ regular, B is format-
sensitive only. C reference: v1 die/adv not yet run (TBD from 7400571),
v2 adds both.
