**Interpretation:** Raw norms are nearly identical (1.2% difference), but z-normalization amplifies per-dimension distributional differences. Oracle embeddings become 23.5% higher norm post-normalization, highly correlated (cosine 0.58), and have structured per-dimension offsets (dim_mean_spread 0.93 vs 0.02). The model can trivially identify oracle samples from their post-normalization embedding pattern.

## Union normalization experiment

**Hypothesis:** If separability is caused by normalization using training-only stats, computing stats over the union of training + oracle data should reduce separability and improve returns.

**Result — naive union (unweighted):** Negligible effect. Oracle is 0.3% of the union by count, barely shifts stats.

**Result — weighted union (5% oracle weight):** Modest reduction in separability metrics but still collapsed.

### Normalization comparison across three approaches

Weighted union reduced the norm gap from 14.8 to 11.0 (26% reduction) and dim_mean_spread from 0.926 to 0.844 (9% reduction). But oracle loss collapsed even further (0.04 vs 0.07) and oracle entropy hit 0.15 — the model still finds and memorizes oracle data easily.

## Conclusions

1. **OOD golden BC degrades significantly** (18.4→12.3, -33%) even without embeddings. High variance (σ=5.55) — the policy is unstable.
2. **The embedding pathway worsens collapse further** (12.3→7.5, -39% from noaug; 18.4→7.5, -59% from control). With real embeddings, the model memorizes golden data and partitions its policy using embedding-as-source-tag.
3. **Normalization is not the root cause.** Naive union and weighted union give 6.7 and 6.5 respectively — no improvement over train-only norm (7.5). The separability is inherent to the data distributions.
4. **The fundamental problem:** Human and PPO agent visit different game states → Qwen produces structurally different embeddings → the model uses these as a source tag regardless of how we normalize.
