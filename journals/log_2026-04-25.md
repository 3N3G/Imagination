## What the matrix actually shows

1. **Direct, single-action steering wins**: collect/place/avoid + cardinal directions all work. The mechanism doesn't need to be inferential — it just needs the embedding to gradient-shift toward the action class.
2. **Chain prompts (iron-pickaxe, diamond, multi-step survive base) are zero-rate**: 0/60 episodes elicited any iron pickaxe or diamond. The policy lacks the planning bandwidth, and the embedding can't supply it.
3. **Negative steering is asymmetric**: avoid_water and avoid_animals both work, but `target_avoid_stone` doesn't. The asymmetry is **not** about negative-language — it's that stone is necessary for game progression (upgrade tree, sleep enclosure, ladder-blocking), so the prompt's "avoid stone" goal conflicts with its still-included "upgrade to stone tier" rule. Removing the conflicting rules in a re-write is a candidate iteration.
4. **survive_long is the most surprising failure**: same prompt class as die_fast (which works very strongly), opposite direction → opposite of expected behavior. This is consistent with the "policy can't extend its survival skill cap" hypothesis but it's worth probing further — does ANY survive-prompt extend length? (e.g., a simpler "stay near safe areas" prompt without the strict base criteria.)

## Length confounds noted

| cell | length | confound |
|---|---|---|
| target_eat_cow_v2 | 850 (+221) | subsistence loop (more steps to eat same #cows) |
| target_hunt_animals_v2 | 960 (+331) | subsistence loop, but cow_eat does climb |
| target_descend_v2 | 804 (+175) | descending creates safer floors → longer survival |
| direction_* | 320–392 (-237 to -309) | walking-while-dying — fewer steps to act |
| die_fast | 383 (-246) | desired |
| survive_long | 416 (-213) | NOT desired (wrong direction) |

This is why direction cells need `move_share_X = X / (LEFT+RIGHT+UP+DOWN)`,
not raw count. The `move_share_*` metric is length-invariant and absorbs
NOOP/DO drift. Per-step rate `X/length` would also remove the length
confound but doesn't handle the NOOP/DO shift; share-of-cardinal-moves
is the cleanest.

## Iteration v3 — `target_avoid_stone_v3` and `survive_long_v3`

After the matrix landed, I iterated the two clearest prompt-logic
bottlenecks. Both were re-run on `C_grounded_2M` at n=30 to a separate
output dir (`..._specificity_iter`), wandb job 7486443.

**v3 design changes:**
- `target_avoid_stone_v3`: deleted the entire Upgrade Decision Tree
  (the v2 prompt kept "Stone Tier: craft stone pickaxe if you have stone
  and wood", which contradicted the avoid-stone goal). Also deleted the
  "mine a tunnel into a cluster of stone" sleep hint. Replaced exploration
  with "explore wood-tier territory" and forbids stone/coal/iron/diamond
  mining + stone-tier crafting + PLACE_STONE.
- `survive_long_v3`: dropped the strict "establish a base with water +
  cow + stone within 3 tiles" criteria. Replaced with a flat priority
  list: keep intrinsics ≥7, NOOP when safe, run from enemies within 4
  tiles, never descend.

**Final v3 vs v2 (baseline shown for reference):**

| cell | n | stone | length | return |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **baseline (no prompt)** | 50 | 17.9 ± 2.2 | 629 ± 79 | 14.66 ± 0.83 |
| target_avoid_stone_v2 | 30 | 19.8 ± 3.1 (z=+0.5, **wrong-way**) | 527 ± 79 (z=-0.9) | 13.63 ± 0.95 |
| target_avoid_stone_v3 | 30 | **12.4 ± 2.6 (z=-1.6, WIN)** | 502 ± 82 (z=-1.1) | 11.67 ± 1.17 |
| survive_long_v2 | 30 | 14.2 ± 2.7 | **416 ± 50 (z=-2.3, wrong-way)** | 12.53 ± 1.34 |
| survive_long_v3 | 30 | 18.3 ± 3.3 | **669 ± 119 (z=+0.3, NULL)** | 14.77 ± 1.12 |

**Conclusions:**

1. **`avoid_stone_v3` flipped from wrong-way to a clear WIN.** Removing
   the contradictory Stone Tier upgrade rule was the fix. Stone mining
   dropped from 17.9 → 12.4/ep (-31% absolute, z=-1.6). The Gemini text
   for v2 *did* express avoidance ("the player will avoid stone") but
   contradicted itself in early steps with "needs to gather stone to
   craft stone tools"; v3 has no such contradiction. **Confirms: the
   policy CAN read negative steering when the prompt is internally
   consistent.**
2. **`survive_long_v3` flipped from wrong-way to NULL (parity with
   baseline).** The simpler prompt no longer disrupts the policy
   (length 416 → 669, return 12.5 → 14.8 ≈ baseline 14.7). But it
   doesn't *extend* episodes either — z=+0.3 is null. **The policy's
   survival skill cap is not a function of the prompt's clarity; it's a
   policy fidelity ceiling.** The base policy already survives ~629
   steps when undisturbed, and any survival-focused embedding can at
   best match that, not exceed it.
3. **Asymmetry**: positive steering (collect_stone, place_stone, descend,
   hunt_animals, all 4 directions) and direct opposites (avoid_animals,
   avoid_water, avoid_stone-v3, die_fast) work. Survive-extending prompts
   don't. This points to a *headroom asymmetry*: it is easier to push the
   policy off its survival routine than to push it further along it.
