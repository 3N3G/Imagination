### Is iron/diamond just one lucky seed?

User's question. Per-episode breakdown shows **NO — 4 distinct
episodes** unlock iron/diamond:

| ep | return | length | unlocks |
|---|---|---|---|
| 24 | 29.10 | 1144 | iron_pickaxe + enter_dungeon |
| 26 | 19.10 | 1210 | iron_sword |
| 27 | 37.10 | 1161 | iron_pickaxe + **iron_armour** + enter_dungeon |
| 28 | 31.10 | 1389 | **collect_diamond** + enter_dungeon |

**ALL 4 require length ≥ 1144 steps**. Iron-tier execution needs
sustained survival (find iron → build furnace → smelt → craft),
typically 800+ steps minimum.

**But the *mean* length isn't longer**: 619.6 (thresh6) vs ~629
(baseline). What changed is the **tail**:

```
length distribution (thresh6 n=30)
[  100, 200):  2 ##
[  200, 300):  3 ###
[  300, 500): 11 ###########
[  500, 800):  6 ######
[  800,1200):  4 ####
[ 1200,inf ):  4 ####    ← iron-tier territory
```

vs baseline where ~0-1 episodes reach 1100+. The std (7.30) is also
much higher than baseline (5.66) — distribution is more bimodal:
either a long deep run that reaches iron, or a short death.

### Mechanism

The threshold-6 + canonical-ordering prompt isn't keeping the average
episode alive longer. It's giving the policy a **better recipe for
the long-survival cases** that already exist in the policy's
distribution. When a run survives past ~1000 steps, the prompt now
contains explicit guidance on intrinsic maintenance order (drink first,
then food, then energy), which apparently lets the policy execute
the iron-tier chain instead of getting stuck in maintenance loops.

The policy's *probability* of long survival is unchanged; conditional
on long survival, the *value* of long survival is higher.

### Take

Threshold-6 trades ~0.7 raw return (within noise) for a fatter
high-quality tail. Best of both worlds would be:
1. Adopt thresh6's canonical-ordering paragraph in v3
2. But keep v2's behavior elsewhere where it's net-positive
3. OR run both prompts in parallel + ensemble

For SCALING_C the lesson is bigger: this proves the C policy *can*
execute iron-tier when prompted properly, even on data without iron
demonstrations. The bottleneck for iron+ is not the architecture —
it's the prompt + survival-frequency.

### SCALING_C verdict (preliminary)

Headline raw return is **lower** for C_v3 (12.30 with thresh6) than
C_grounded_2M (17.63 with thresh6). On the headline metric, SCALING_C
made things worse.

But the **achievement frontier moved**: C_v3 unlocks deeper crafting
chains (iron→diamond→diamond_pickaxe) at higher rates than any prior C.
The training data quality lift (PPO-RNN max RTG 28.4 vs old 17.1) shows
up not as raw-return lift but as **capability depth** — the policy
*can* do iron+diamond crafting, it just needs the right prompt and
loses survival reliability in the trade.

Mechanistic theory: PPO-RNN training data has mean episode length ~317;
the new C_v3 inherited that "shorter, denser, deeper" pattern instead
of C_grounded_2M's "longer, sparser, shallower". Two follow-ups:
1. **β sweep**: try lower AWR β (=3, =1) in stage 0 to weaken the
   PPO-RNN signal, see if anchoring shifts back toward C_grounded_2M
   length while keeping the deep-chain unlocks.
2. **Mixed dataset**: train on `top-2M PSF v2 ⊕ top-4M PPO-RNN v3`
   (6M combined) — gets both survival reliability and crafting depth.
3. **Higher oracle weight**: increase `--oracle-loss-weight` from 0.5
   to 1.0 in stage 1 — should anchor more strongly to C-style golden
   behavior (which is long-survival).
