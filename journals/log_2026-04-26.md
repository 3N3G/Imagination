## Why does C take so much longer to do the BASIC chain?

The BASIC chain (wood → table → pickaxe → stone → table+furnace →
stone-tier tools) is the same on both policies in *steps required to
execute*. But C delays it ~100 steps:

1. **NOOP rate 5.3% vs 0.4%.** Over 600 steps, C noops ~32 times.
   PPO-RNN noops ~1 time over 318 steps. 32 wasted steps is roughly
   the gap between when C and PPO finish wood-tier tools.
2. **C revisits the same achievement** (e.g., adjusts position
   between mining attempts more than needed). Anecdotally from the
   gemini logs of long C episodes: it re-evaluates with the same
   "go to nearest tree" plan after several intermediate steps that
   could have been a single craft.
3. **Idle "level-up attempts"** — C tries LEVEL_UP_STR/DEX/INT 2.6%
   of total actions vs PPO's 0.8%. With no XP these are no-ops too
   (XP comes from descending floors, which C does 12% of the time).
   So C burns ~16 actions per 600-step episode trying to spend XP it
   doesn't have.

Combined "wasted action" budget on C: ~50 steps per episode out of
600. That alone accounts for ~2 lost achievements at PPO-RNN's
6.54-per-100-steps rate.

## Diagnostic: what's the binding constraint?

Three candidate explanations for why C falls 13 raw return short:

### (A) Inefficiency on Floor 0 — wasted actions
C noops 13× more, level-up-attempts 3× more, has the
~5%-of-action overhead. This costs ~50 steps per episode of wasted
budget. Order of magnitude: ~2-3 raw return points if those steps
were re-purposed to BASIC achievement collection.

### (B) Doesn't descend → can't reach Floor 1 INTERMEDIATE band
C reaches Floor 1 12% of episodes, PPO-RNN 56%. The Floor 1 +3
achievements (find_bow, open_chest, fire_bow, eat_snail,
drink_potion, defeat_orc_solider, plus more enter_dungeon) total
**+7.82 raw return** of headroom (per the SCALING_C gap table).
This is the dominant gap.

### (C) Iron-tier execution
C never crafts make_iron_pickaxe / make_iron_sword. PPO-RNN gets
26% / 24% on these. Worth ~+0.6 raw return — small.

**Total accounting**: ~3 (A) + 7.8 (B) + 0.6 (C) ≈ +11.4 raw, which
matches the observed gap of +11.46 almost exactly. So the model is:
*"C wastes ~3 points to inefficiency on Floor 0, and another 8
points to never seeing the Floor 1 content".*

## Why doesn't C descend?

C's training data (top-2M-PSF) was generated by a PPO that itself
descends only ~12% of episodes. AWR + BC trained on that data
imitates the behavior. The policy never SEES (in training data)
trajectories where descent happens regularly + survives floor 1
events.

Even when prompts try to push descent (`target_descend_v2` lifts
enter_dungeon to 27%, score-max v2 lifts it to 39%), the policy can
only push so far before its underlying skill cap shows: it doesn't
know what to do once it's on Floor 1. The Floor-1 INTERMEDIATE
achievements (find_bow at 7%, open_chest at 7%) stay tiny even when
descent is up at 39%, because the policy never trained on Floor 1.

This is the precise mechanism SCALING_C addresses: feed C training
data from PPO-RNN 1e8 — which actually descends 56% and harvests the
Floor 1 +3 cluster — and AWR will imitate THAT behavior. Expected
post-SCALING-C C: 18-20 raw baseline + score-max v2 prompt → 22-26.
Same recipe, different data, +6-8 raw return.

## Why doesn't C noop less?

C's source PPO trajectories presumably contained NOOP actions in
their distribution. AWR weighs trajectories by advantage, but
NOOP is "low cost, easy to learn" so even a small NOOP frequency
in the data gets imitated. The PPO-RNN at 1e8 has trained itself
out of NOOP via reward signal — every step has expected value.

Not addressable via prompt steering — would need either (a) AWR
training that strongly downweights NOOP-following actions, or (b)
training data without NOOPs (which PPO-RNN-derived data should have
much less of).

## What's NOT the binding constraint

- **Gemini prediction quality.** Gemini correctly identifies threats
  in C's dying episodes. The policy doesn't act on them.
- **Embedding fidelity.** AWR-only checkpoint gets 17.67 (close to
  freezenone+v2's 18.33). Dropping the BC+oracle finetune doesn't
  push past 18 either. The bottleneck isn't the embedding pathway
  but the underlying policy's skill repertoire.
- **Prompt iteration.** v1=15.51, v2=18.33, v3=15.30, v4=12.73. v2
  is the local ceiling. More prompts can't unlock floor-1 behavior
  the policy doesn't have.

### What this tells us

1. **Train/eval mismatch IS real but small.** Closing the
   inference-time gap between "Gemini sees the actual future"
   (training distribution) and "Gemini just describes the present"

   alone cannot explain the 11.46-point gap to PPO-RNN.

2. **Variance collapse is the headline.** Std drops from 5.66 → 3.09
   (close to half). With future-state info, the policy's runs are
   much more uniform — the high-variance "die early" tail goes away.

   the prompt repeatedly reminds the policy "yes, you'll still be

## NOOP investigation (closed by source-code inspection)

The replay analysis showed C uses NOOP **5.3%** of actions vs PPO-RNN
**0.4%**. Initial thought: C is "stalling" voluntarily. Investigation
proved otherwise — Craftax forces NOOP every step the player is asleep
or resting. Evidence in `craftax/craftax/game_logic.py:3018-3020`:

```python
# Interrupt action if sleeping or resting
action = jax.lax.select(state.is_sleeping, Action.NOOP.value, action)
action = jax.lax.select(state.is_resting, Action.NOOP.value, action)
```

C's higher NOOP rate is a **sleep-frequency proxy**, not a behavioral
pathology. Consistent with C's 52% wake_up rate vs PPO-RNN's 40%, and
with the score-max v2 prompt elevating SLEEP for the +1 wake_up
achievement. **No NOOP-mask sampling eval needed** — masking the logit
would have no effect because the env still overrides any emitted action
to NOOP during the sleep cycle. Documented as a new section in
`docs/DEATH_ANALYSIS.md` and saved to memory as
`feedback_craftax_noop_forced_sleep.md`.

This **subtracts ~3 raw return** from the previously-attributed "Floor 0
inefficiency / NOOP overhead" line of the gap-breakdown — the NOOP
overhead is *survival behavior*, not waste. Updated breakdown:

| component | est. cost in raw return | new evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Inference-time Gemini quality (train/eval) | ~2.0 | oracle-future +1.97 (above) |
| Floor 0 inefficiency from sleep cycles | ~0 | env-forced; not optimization room |
| Never seeing Floor 1 INTERMEDIATE band | ~7.0 | per-achievement gap |
| Iron-tier execution | ~0.6 | per-achievement gap |
| Subtotal accounted | ~9.6 | of 11.46 observed |
| Residual (other inefficiencies) | ~1.9 | uncharacterized |

The trajectory-data bottleneck remains the dominant story, but the gap
isn't quite as concentrated on Floor 0 inefficiency as the first
multi-hour analysis suggested.

## SCALING_C Phase 1 cadence-bias bug + recovery

The original Phase 1 PPO-RNN trajectory save (job 7503746, completed
2026-04-26 01:11) used `--save_traj_every 10 --save_traj_start_step 500`,
producing 103 saved batches × 65,536 transitions = 6.7 M raw samples
(3.3 GB compressed).

**Critical bug discovered when scanning the data**: per-completed-episode
return mean = **1.09**, median = **−0.10**, max single-episode = 30.10.
This sharply contradicts the wandb training-time mean of 27.87 (and
the Phase 6 video eval of 26.12).

**Root cause**: `--save_traj_every 10` saves 1 batch every 10 update
steps. Episodes average ~317 timesteps; one update step = 64 timesteps
× 1024 envs. P(an episode ends within a saved batch) ≈ 64/317 ≈ 20%
per env, but those episodes' STARTS were almost always in the unsaved
9-batch gap before. So the only "completed" episodes visible in the
saved data are *partial tails* — typically the last 1–60 steps of an
otherwise long episode, dominated by the death penalty.

The 60% of visible episodes with return ∈ [−1, 0) are precisely those
tails: a few negative-reward steps before death. Picking "top-K rows
by RTG" requires knowing each row's forward rewards through episode
end, which span unsaved batches → the data is unusable as-is.

**Fix**: re-run PPO-RNN with `--save_traj_every 1
--save_traj_start_step 1000` (continuous save through last 525 batches,
~17 GB final, ~34 M transitions). Job 7507785 completed at 14:07,
producing all 525 batches (1000 → 1524) on disk.

`--num_envs 1024` flag added to `pipeline/filter_and_repack.py` to
support PPO-RNN's flat 2D NPZ format (vs the legacy default of 128).
