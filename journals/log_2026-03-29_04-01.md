# Mar 29 – Apr 1: Ablations & BC+AWR Genesis

## Predict-State-Only Results (Mar 29) — FAILED

Top-250 episodes, gemini-3.1-flash-lite predict-only labels.

| Model | Return | Std |
|-------|--------|-----|
| ps-w512 | 7.20 | 4.83 |
| ps-w1024 | 9.40 | 5.93 |
| ps-w2048 | 6.60 | 4.20 |

All terrible. Hypotheses: flash-lite too weak, top-250 filtering too aggressive, or predict-only labels less informative than oracle.

## PSF Full-Pipeline Rerun (Mar 30) — SUCCESS

Used full Gemini 2.5 Flash (not flash-lite) on same data. Predict-state-only labels with the good model.

| Model | Return | Std |
|-------|--------|-----|
| psf-w512 | 16.70 | 4.80 |
| psf-w1024 | 16.90 | 4.87 |
| **psf-w2048** | **18.90** | **1.40** |

Matches oracle baselines. **Confirms: predict-only works with good enough Gemini model.** The 3.1-flash-lite was the problem, not the predict-only approach.

| adversarial | 14.58 |

**psf-w2048 (predict-trained):**

## BC+AWR v2 Sweep (Mar 31)

First attempt at BC+AWR: joint oracle head that learns to predict using hidden states while training the policy with AWR.

4 variants (all w512, no LayerNorm/Dropout):
| Variant | oracle_frac | oracle_weight | entropy | anneal |
|---------|-------------|---------------|---------|--------|
| gentle | 0.05 | 1.0 | 0.0 | No |
| entropy | 0.10 | 2.0 | 0.01 | No |
| oawr | 0.10 | 1.0 | 0.0 | No |
| anneal | 0.25 | 5.0 | 0.005 | Yes |

Training completed. Validation job ran but results not captured (architecture mismatch).

Introduced `train_awr_weighted_v2.py`: added LayerNorm, Dropout, oracle discrimination head (predict real vs zero context).

## 2x2 Factorial (Apr 1)

Gemini model × label type, trained + zero-embed eval:

| Experiment | Return (zero-embed) |
|------------|-------------------|
| 25-flash predict-only | 13.89 ± 6.24 |
| 31-lite oracle | 11.85 ± 5.97 |

Both worse than full-pipeline models (16-19). Smaller/cheaper Gemini degrades quality.

## Reflections

1. **Gemini model quality matters more than label type.** 2.5 Flash >> 3.1-flash-lite, regardless of oracle vs predict-only.
2. **Embedding ablations show models aren't deeply using semantic content.** All models are relatively robust to garbage embeddings — suggests the hidden branch acts more as a bias term than a semantic signal.
3. **BC+AWR oracle head is the right direction** to force the model to actually learn from context. Need to validate discrimination (real vs zero acc) translates to gameplay.
