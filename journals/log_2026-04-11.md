## Experiment 1: Embedding Method Comparison

**Motivation:** Qwen3-8B is a generative model. Its layer-30 mean-pooled hidden states
carry semantic information, but they also carry a lot of generation-specific structure
(positional patterns, token prediction state) that may add noise relative to what the
policy needs. A model trained explicitly for embedding might produce cleaner, more
semantically concentrated representations.

**Three conditions** (same flash-lite PSF labels, same training setup, pure AWR):

## Experiment 2: History-Conditioned Imagination (hist5)

**Motivation:** With snapshot-only PSF prompts, Gemini predicts future states without
any knowledge of what the agent has been doing. Its predictions may be plausible in
general but not grounded in the agent's actual trajectory — the imagination may not
correspond well to the actions the agent actually takes. Passing the last 5 (obs,
action, reward) tuples before the current state gives Gemini context about the agent's
recent behavior, which may produce predictions that are more consistent with the
agent's decision-making and therefore more useful as a conditioning signal.

**Setup:** Same Qwen3-8B embedding and pure AWR training, but labels generated with
k=5 history context prepended. All 158 filtered_trajectories files labelled (~4.27M
calls, complete). Currently running embedding → merge → train → eval 50ep. ETA ~2 days.
