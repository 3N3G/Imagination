# 2026-04-16

## Context

Apr-12 Experiment 1 ("freeze BC, 4 configs") was trained on **oracle-labelled** Gemini data (the Gemini prompt saw 15 future steps). At online eval time the embedder has no future, so embeddings are drawn from the **predict-state-only (PSF)** distribution. The freeze_obs_bcawr policy reached 16.80 ± 3.49 despite this train/eval mismatch, but Apr-12 Exp 4 (adversarial prompts) and Exp 8 (step-0 counterfactuals) showed the policy is content-insensitive — the oracle/PSF shift is invisible because it doesn't read content.

Today's question: does it matter *at all* whether the labelling pipeline is PSF-consistent end-to-end? And if we vary the text-encoder under a PSF-consistent pipeline, does any encoder start extracting more-useful content?

## Experiment 15 — PSF-consistent freeze BC+AWR

### Setup

Training data (125 files PSF, gemini-3.1-flash-lite, predict-state-only labels) already existed with three embeddings: qwen3gen (Qwen3-8B layer-30, 4096-d), qwen3emb (Qwen3-Embedding-8B last-token, 4096-d), and gemini_emb (gemini-embedding-001, 3072-d).

Golden/BC PSF data existed only for qwen3gen (`oracle_pipeline/predict_only_final/`). Re-embedded the same PSF Gemini texts with qwen3emb and gemini_emb to produce matching 4096-d and 3072-d golden datasets.

Pretrained AWR-only PSF checkpoints existed for all three encoders from Apr-12 (`awr_psf_{qwen3gen,qwen3emb,gemini_emb}/final.pth`). Freeze fine-tune starts from those, following the Apr-12 Exp-1 recipe:

- freeze `obs_branch` (or `obs_and_post_merge`), 50K steps, LR=1e-4, entropy=0.01, grad-clip 1.0
- BC-only configs: `--no-awr --oracle-loss-weight 1.0`
- BC+AWR configs: `--oracle-fraction 0.05 --oracle-loss-weight 0.5`

### 15b — Encoder sweep on freeze_obs_bcawr (qwen3gen, qwen3emb, gemini_emb)

Same hyperparameters, same golden PSF (matched dim per encoder), same frozen-obs + BC+AWR recipe. Only the encoder (and therefore the embedding distribution) changes.

#### Golden PSF validation

| Encoder | acc_real | acc_zero | acc_shuf | Δ(real−zero) | Δ(real−shuf) |
|---------|---------:|---------:|---------:|-------------:|-------------:|
| qwen3gen   | 79.57% |  9.75% | 69.65% | +69.82pp |  +9.92pp |
| qwen3emb   | **83.28%** | 11.62% | 68.03% | **+71.66pp** | **+15.25pp** |
| gemini_emb | 80.10% | 11.06% | 71.76% | +69.04pp |  +8.34pp |

qwen3emb shows the largest real-vs-shuffled gap (+15.3pp) — highest content-sensitivity on validation.

#### Online eval (50 ep)

| Encoder | Return | Apr-12 pure-AWR ref |
|---------|-------:|--------------------:|
| qwen3gen   | **17.58 ± 3.56** | 18.62 |
| qwen3emb   | 16.60 ± 4.63 | 17.86 |
| gemini_emb | 14.96 ± 5.11 | 18.80 |

**Ordering reverses pure-AWR trend.** Apr-12 pure AWR: gemini_emb (18.80) ≥ qwen3gen (18.62) > qwen3emb (17.86), all within noise. Freeze BC+AWR: qwen3gen (17.58) > qwen3emb (16.60) > gemini_emb (14.96), ~2.6pp spread.

### Verdict

- **PSF-consistent pipeline buys +0.78 on the best freeze config** (17.58 vs 16.80). Directionally positive but within the run-to-run noise band (±3.5). Matches the expectation that the content-insensitive policy would be mostly invariant to the oracle→PSF shift.
- **Pure-BC configs diverge under PSF:**
  - `obs_bc` collapses harder (2.96 vs 5.54) — the "oracle-ish embedding ⇒ oracle action" shortcut, which pure BC leaned on under oracle labels, no longer exists when training and eval embeddings come from the same (PSF) distribution.
  - `all_bc` improves (12.74 vs 8.56) — with both branches frozen except the hidden projection, BC has less room to overfit oracle, and the obs pathway carries more of the remaining signal.
- **Encoder ordering under freeze_obs_bcawr differs from pure AWR.** The dedicated embedding models (qwen3emb, gemini_emb) didn't help. qwen3gen marginally best; gemini_emb noticeably worst (~2.6pp below qwen3gen). Unclear whether this is a per-encoder effect or a seed-level artifact at 50 eps.
- **Still not beating unaug 18.38.** The closest freeze result (17.58 qwen3gen) is within noise of unaug but below it. The imagination pathway still does not add net value online; the freeze+AWR lever gets us close to the obs-only baseline without overshooting it.
- **qwen3emb had the largest real-vs-shuffled validation gap (+15.3pp)** but did not translate into the highest online return. Consistent with Apr-12 Exp 8 — validation content-sensitivity is not a reliable predictor of online content usage.

### What this does not close

- We only probed the obs-frozen BC+AWR config on all three encoders (one setting each). A wider sweep (β, oracle_weight, longer training) on qwen3emb/gemini_emb could still find a point where dedicated embeddings outperform the generative layer-30 readout.
- Online content-sensitivity (counterfactual direction-match) was not re-run for the PSF versions. The improved real-vs-shuffled gap for qwen3emb is only a validation-time signal.
- The 2.6pp encoder spread on a single 50-ep eval is at the edge of noise; a replicate or held-out eval would tighten the ordering.
