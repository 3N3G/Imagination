### Interpretation

- **Same recipe, fresh labels, +2.62 return.** Holding β, ofrac, ow, freeze, lr, arch, and pretrained-init schedule constant, the only changes between Apr-17 gemini_emb freezenone and v2 are: (i) `obs_to_text` now emits equipment fields, (ii) gameplay.py survivefix + stations fixes, (iii) labeller upgraded from 2.5-flash to 3.1-flash. The lift is larger than all previous gemini_emb recipe sweeps combined (Apr-17 Exp 19's 8 configs had a 4.4pp spread and only one config above baseline).
- **First parity with unaug 18.38 for any augmented policy.** Previously every augmented policy was ≥0.5pp below unaug; v2 is +0.44 above (within 1σ but the *direction* has flipped for the first time since model-size sweep).
- **Length 702 ± 516** is long — the policy is surviving, not just speed-running. Achievement count 19/28 ≈ matches qwen3gen pattern.
- The Apr-17 Exp 20 HP/food probe showed gemini_emb had the cleanest correct-sign content-to-V mapping even pre-fix; the v2 lift is consistent with that signal finally landing on a policy that can act on it, now that the imagination text describes the player's actual equipment.

### What this does not close

- **We have not re-run the content probes on the v2 checkpoint.** The +2.62 lift could come from (a) richer imagination content unlocking better hidden-pathway usage, or (b) the stations+survivefix prompts producing narratives that are just obs-independent strategy nuggets the policy latches onto through the obs branch. HP/food perturbation + die/adversarial prompt probes on v2 would localize this.
- **Only the freezenone recipe was re-tried.** β, ofrac, ow, freeze sweeps from Apr-17 have not been repeated on the fixed pipeline — the v2 surface could look very different (e.g., the encoder-specific preferences for β=30 vs β=3 may shift).
- **qwen3gen and qwen3emb have not been re-run on fixed labels.** Apr-17 ranked qwen3emb > qwen3gen > gemini_emb by return (17.88 / 17.58 / 16.20). v2 flipped gemini_emb to top; whether the other two also benefit is open.
- **50 eps, std 3.89** — a 100-ep rerun would tighten the comparison to unaug noticeably (SE 0.55 → 0.39).

## Experiment 25 — Cadence-prompt mismatch discovery + fix

**Error found:** the prompt template explicitly asks Gemini to forecast "the next FIVE steps … Do not forecast beyond five time steps", but every training and eval run up to this point was calling Gemini once every **15** environment steps. Consequence: the policy reads a 5-step forecast that then sits stale for the next 14 steps until the next refresh.

Three constants were out of sync:
- `pipeline/config.py` → `GEMINI_STEP_CADENCE = 15` (labelling side)
- `eval/eval_online.py` → `STEP_CADENCE = 15` (online eval)
- `offline_rl/train_ppo_augmented.py` → `STEP_CADENCE = 15` (PPO online)

All three were updated to `5`. This is a **system-level fix** that invalidates the direct interpretability of every prior cadence=15 result against the prompt template semantics. However, the existing Apr-18 v2 checkpoint (18.82) trained on cadence=15 labels and is still a valid baseline under that labelling regime; the new run will produce a fresh checkpoint under the prompt-aligned regime.

## Experiment 26 — Gemini thinking-budget truncation bug

**Error found:** during online eval with `gemini-3-flash-preview`, the `full_text` responses were coming back as a single sentence of State Understanding with no Prediction section, despite 1806-token call counts. Root cause traced to `eval/eval_online.py:391`:

```python
_use_thinking = _gemini_model.startswith("gemini-2.5")
```

The flag name is inverted — `_use_thinking=True` actually *disables* Gemini thinking by explicitly setting `thinkingConfig={"thinkingBudget": 0}`. The check was written when 2.5-flash was the only model needing explicit suppression; for `gemini-3-flash-preview` it evaluates `False`, so `thinkingBudget=0` was never sent and Gemini 3 defaulted to thinking ON, consuming the 512-token `maxOutputTokens` cap on hidden reasoning before any visible output was emitted.

**Fix applied:** extended the check to match `gemini-3*` as well. The 1806-token figure the user observed is `promptTokenCount`, not `candidatesTokenCount` — completion was truncated. (`pipeline/gemini_label.py` is unaffected: the labelling scripts explicitly pass `--thinking-budget 0` on the command line.)

## Experiment 27 — Cadence=5 pipeline, first attempt (cancelled) + isolation rule

Initial cadence=5 chain (jobs 7236096..7236102) was submitted to write fresh labels into the **existing** Apr-18 label directory `gemini_labels_psf_v2_3flash`, relying on `gemini_label.py`'s resumable-per-`sample_idx` logic to fill in new offsets (5, 10, 20, 25, …) while reusing existing labels at (0, 15, 30, …).

**Error caught:** those existing labels were generated on 2026-04-18 09:10 — before a 2026-04-18 12:00 revision of the prompt template that added two wording refinements (survival: "avoid arrows, and avoid enemies unless you can kill them"; crafting: "Crafting anything requires being adjacent to a Crafting Table"). Result: the proposed cadence=5 dataset would have been **~50% old-prompt labels / ~50% current-prompt labels** — two labelling methods silently mixed in one training set.

**Resolution:** entire cadence=5 chain cancelled. New rule recorded: each labelling method change (cadence, prompt, model, obs_to_text, etc.) writes to its OWN output directory. Never rely on resume to "fill in" labels across method boundaries.

### Result

**50-ep mean return = 18.58 ± 3.51** (min=5.10, max=29.10, length=645 ± 381, achievements=18.7 ± 3.0).

Validation on golden (final step, 2026-04-21 freezenone):
- acc_real = 0.9105
- acc_zero = 0.8297
- Δ(real-zero) = +0.0807
- KL(real||zero) = 0.164 (higher than Apr-19's 0.035)

**Comparison against cadence=15 baseline (Apr-19 Exp 22, same recipe):**

| Metric | Cadence=15 (Apr-19) | Cadence=5 (Apr-21) | Δ |
|---|---|---|---|
| 50-ep return | 18.82 ± 3.89 | 18.58 ± 3.51 | −0.24 (within 1σ) |
| acc_real | 0.907 | 0.9105 | +0.004 |
| Δ(real-zero) on val | +0.074 | +0.081 | +0.007 |
| KL(real\|\|zero) | 0.035 | 0.164 | ×4.7 higher |

**Interpretation.** The fresh-horizon prompt alignment did **not** move the online return — cadence=5 and cadence=15 are statistically indistinguishable at n=50. This is the cleanest test so far that the policy's behavior on the imagination branch is not tied to the temporal freshness of the forecast.

Three notable shifts despite the null return:
1. **KL(real||zero) is 4.7× larger** on the cadence=5 checkpoint. The policy's logits differ more between real and zeroed imagination than under cadence=15 — the branch is being *read* more actively, even though the reading does not translate into online return.
2. **Δ(real-zero) on val is preserved** at +0.08, suggesting the policy is still extracting the "content is present vs absent" signal on-policy matches.
3. **Freezenone val accuracy hits 0.91** — same as cadence=15 — indicating the val-set behavior is saturated for this recipe regardless of cadence.

The cadence=5 content-probe array (7324265, die + adversarial) is now in flight and will confirm whether the elevated KL represents content-sensitivity or just higher logit variance to the presence/absence of the embedding.

### Cross-cadence comparison

| Mode | Cadence=15 (Apr-19 Exp 24) | Cadence=5 (Apr-21) |
|---|---|---|
| Baseline | 18.82 ± 3.89 | 18.58 ± 3.51 |
| Die | 18.84 ± 4.50 (Δ≈0) | 18.48 ± 3.67 (Δ=−0.10) |
| Adversarial | 18.42 ± 3.09 (Δ≈−0.4) | 17.58 ± 3.31 (Δ=−1.00) |

**Interpretation — content-invariance holds under fresh-horizon alignment.** Despite KL(real||zero) being 4.7× higher on cadence=5, the online return under die and adversarial prompts remains within noise of the real-gemini baseline. The policy's logit geometry is more responsive to imagination *perturbation* (zeroing vs. real, shuffling vs. real show measurable KL shifts) but the resulting argmax action distribution does not flip in ways that change return.

Three refinements of the earlier interpretation:
1. **Val-set acc_real lift ≠ online behavior change.** Val Δ(real-zero) of +0.081 on cadence=5 is not zero, meaning the policy *does* agree with oracle actions more often when given real content on validation states. But neither die nor adversarial content degrade online reward materially, so whichever extra matches the policy finds on-policy do not compound into a different trajectory.
2. **Adversarial is the only mode with consistent negative signal** across both cadences (cadence=15: −0.4; cadence=5: −1.0). The direction is consistent but the magnitude is weak. If the policy were truly using imagination content, an adversarial prompt should be much more damaging. The weakness is evidence for "very limited use", not "no use".
3. **Fresh horizon did not unlock the imagination pathway.** We can now rule out "the policy ignores imagination because the forecast is stale" as the mechanism. Both stale-cadence=15 and fresh-cadence=5 models give the same content-probe pattern. The bottleneck is in the *conditioning architecture* or the *gradient signal*, not the temporal alignment of the forecast.
