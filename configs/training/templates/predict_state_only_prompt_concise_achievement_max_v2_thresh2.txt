Craftax overview:
Craftax is a game about exploring dungeons, mining, crafting and fighting enemies. Each one-time achievement (collect a resource, craft an item, defeat a foe, descend a floor, etc.) adds +1 to the score. The player wants to unlock as many DIFFERENT achievements as possible across one episode.

1. Target descend
The player moves directly toward any visible open ladder and uses DESCEND as soon as the player is standing on it. If no ladder is visible, the player walks toward the most ladder-likely unexplored direction. On non-overworld floors where the ladder is closed, the player engages mobs along the route until 8 are killed and the ladder opens, then descends. The player only mines through stone walls when they block the shortest path to the ladder. Each new floor entered grants both +1 enter_X achievement AND a player_xp the player can spend on Strength / Dexterity / Intelligence.

2. Survive
The player must track health, food, drink, and energy. Maintenance thresholds are kept generous so the player never lets an intrinsic actually run out: if food is <= 2, get food immediately by killing animals and eating them. If drink is <= 2, get drink immediately from water tiles. If energy is <= 2, make a safe enclosure and SLEEP — sleep also unlocks +1 wake_up. If health is <= 2, restore food, drink, and energy before doing anything risky. To avoid losing health, avoid arrows, and avoid enemies unless you can kill them. The player should never sleep in the open. Before sleeping, block enemies out, for example with stone walls. An easy way for the player to become safe is to mine a tunnel into a cluster of stone and place a stone behind blocking off the tunnel.

Canonical maintenance ordering. The intrinsics deplete at different rates in Craftax. Drink is the fastest-depleting intrinsic (the player loses 1 unit of drink roughly every 20 game steps), food is next (about every 25 steps), and energy is the slowest (about every 30 steps). Whenever multiple intrinsics are below the threshold above, the player should service them in canonical order from fastest-depleting to slowest — that is, drink first, then food, then energy — because the fastest-depleting one will hit zero first and start eating health. More precisely, the player should compute, for each below-threshold intrinsic, how close it is to zero relative to its depletion rate, and address whichever one is currently most-depleted relative to its rate before returning to descent or any opportunistic milestone.

3. One-shot opportunistic milestones
Each item below is a one-time +1 achievement the baseline policy often misses. The player should grab one when it is reachable in the next 1–3 steps, but NEVER detour for it more than 3 tiles off the descent path.
  (a) collect_sapling — when a small green sapling icon is visible on a grass tile within 3 tiles, walk to it and DO to harvest. Saplings are easy +1.
  (b) place_plant — once the player holds at least 1 sapling AND is standing on a grass tile (or one is adjacent), use PLACE_PLANT immediately. The plant ripens after roughly 30–60 steps. Free +1.
  (c) eat_plant — if the player passes within 1 tile of an own placed plant that is now fruit-bearing (a bright fruit icon on top), DO to eat. Free +1, also restores food.
  (d) place_torch — once the player has crafted at least 1 torch (1 Wood + 1 Coal at a crafting table), use PLACE_TORCH on the current tile. The very first one in the episode is +1.
None of these milestones are worth dying for or significantly delaying descent for. They are pickups along the way.

If the ladder is open and on screen, the player should prioritize using it unless they are in the overworld. The overworld has the majority of the resources so the player should first acquire at least stone tools before leaving. Note that open and visible are not the same; an open ladder can be used, but the player still needs to find it (down_ladder) to use it. On later floors, the ladder opens only after 8 troops have been killed.
